QR IWTER-OFFICE MEMORANDUM

SUBJECT: Evaluation of Added Flavor DATE: August 23, 1983
in Cigarette Products
TO: Dr. D. L. Roberts FROM: Debbie A, Garrett
Flavor Technology
Applied R&D

It has been proposed that consumer testing be used to quantify the
overall contribution of flavors. The effects of plasticizer, top dressing and
casing depend heavily on elgarette design. If only a few of the major factors
that affect smoke or flavor delivery like filter efficiency, air dilution and
blend make-up are examined quite a few products will need to be designed and
made, ‘The availability of the resources necessary to make and test product
limits our ability to evaluate flavor and include the parameters of cigarette
design. Nevertheless, I believe it is important if we want to assess the
quantitative contribution of flavor realistically that some of the governing
design factors be taken into consideration. If the requirements of a study
needed to make a realistic evaluation of flavor are too overwhelming, our
original idea may need to be reconsidered. It is possible to demonstrate {but
not quantify) the value of added flavor with only a few products. However,
this information may not be that useful to us. I feel at this point that the
original objective along with a feasible test design warrants further
discussion.
