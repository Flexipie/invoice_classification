As we discussed by telephone, cigarettes containing carbonized flue-cured
stems as a portion of the total filler were evaluated by informal laboratory
smoking tests. Cigarettes with 50% of the tobacco replaced with carbonized
flue-cured stens were prepared (L-Test No. 17,183) and evaluated by expert
smoxers in the laboratory. A CAMEL Lights control was used for comparison
smaking. Results shoved that the test cigarette was milder, had less tobacco
taste with a slight stale taste and produced less side-stream smoke when coti~

_ pared to the control CAMEL Lights cigarette. Even though the taste of the
test product was quite different from the control, it was judged to be
surprisingly good for a product with 50} substitution of the tobacco filler.

‘These results are in agreement with a similar test conducted in August,
1981 (L-Test No. 16,794). In this test, carbonized flue-cured stems were used
to replace 30% and 50% of the tobacco filler in the test CAMEL Lights samples.
Laboratory smoking results showed the test products to be milder with less
tobacco taste than a CAMEL Lights control, Off=taste was very low, especially
