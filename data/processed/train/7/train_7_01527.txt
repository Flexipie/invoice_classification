THE FEDERAL GOVERNMENT'S ATTEMPT TO POLICE
ERAGE 1s TH 2) H

Dear Colleague:

I strongly support the goal of reducing underage
smoking, but the idea that the federal government can
regulate the problem out of existence is absurd. Yet
that is exactly what the Clinton Administration and the
FDA have proposed.

The attached cartoon makes the point that the
federal government cannot possibly police individual
decisions made every day by teenagers around the
country. strong family values and education, together
with state laws and local enforcement programs designed
to fit the communities involved, are the only effective
ways to address the problem of underage smoking.

We all know this Administration wants to show how
“gough" it is in ite fight against tobacco. But I say
it’s time to call off the federal regulators and put our

energies behind programs that really work.
