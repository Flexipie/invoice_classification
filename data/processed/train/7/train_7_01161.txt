SUBJECT: Tar Blocker’ Prototypes

Scientific & Regulatory Affairs has reviewed the request to approve a human smoking
behavior/serum nicotine study in the Psychophysiology Laboratory, The study is to
examine the effects of drilling deep ‘hotes' (PD-5145 AB holes) or wide ‘slots’ (PD-5145
AB slots) in the filter instead of the conventional ‘holes’. Twelve subjects will emoke one
cigarette of each type, as well as one of each of two control cigarettes (Vantage Ultra Lt
85 and Now 85 Soft Pack).

Prototype PD-5145 AB has the same blend as cigarette PD-5184 AB which was
previously approved by HRRC (R-9306) for a CLT in Milwaukee with 60 persons, one
pack per person. PD-5184 AB had 56% conventional air dilution, whereas, PD-5145 AB
‘holes’ and PD-5145 AB ‘slots’ have 68% air dilution

‘The FTC Tar, nicotine, CO and puff number have been reviewed for these two
prototypes. The ‘tar’, nicotine and CO vatues are all lower than values for PD-5184 AB.

Scientific & Regulatory Affairs recommends that the human smoking behavior/serum
nicotine study be conducted with PD-5i45 AB ’holes' and PD-5145 AB ‘slots' with 68%
air ditution. Ifthe results of the testing are favorable, expanded human testing would
require that # more extensive chemical testing plan be designed to evaluate cigarette
smoke and condensate from these prototypes, as well as further review by Scientific &
Regulatory Affairs.

Please take this proposal to the HRRC.
