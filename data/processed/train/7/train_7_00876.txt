SUBJECT: Standardization of Industry Terminology

This is in response to your attached memo and Richard Marcotullio's
attached memo on the aforementioned subject.

It is my opinion that R. J. Reynolds Tobacco Company should not
agree with the attached suggestions to change the terminology
describing cigarettes from "tar" to “condensate.”

The low tar category today and in the future is extremely important -
to the total cigarette industry in the United States and is certainly
of equal importance to R. J. Reynolds Tobacco Company. I personally
see no advantage whatsoever in changing this terminology, because it
could attract FTC involvement in this subject; it could confuse the
trade; and certainly in my opinion, would be a major problem for
consumers, Additionally, after many years in the international
cigarette business, I see no advantage at all to attempting to
Standardize industry terminology en a worldwide basis and feel that
this should be left to the lecal country involved,

Therefore, it is my opinion that we should reply to International -
that we would be strongly against any changes such as those recommended,
