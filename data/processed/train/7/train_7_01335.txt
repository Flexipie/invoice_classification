Dear Bob:

This letter is in response to your note regarding the
recent decision to use the soft pack configuration for
the VAT project as opposed to a box package.

In answer to your specific recommendation to test market
a box versus a soft pack concept, our decision is to
develop and test a soft pack VAT product only.

You are correct in atating that a great deal of analysis
has gone into the decision of either a soft pack or a
box for VAT and it is for this reason, historical RIRT
marketing experience, and the unique nature of VAT that
we feel a soft pack approach is the most opportune.

Historically, RIRT haa introduced box packaging on several
branda with very limited success, The opportunities you
point out with regard to demographic and geographic gains
to be derived from a box package have never really
materialized when applied to many other brands.

Essentially, our analysis of this subject has clearly
indicated that the broadest upside potential for a VAT
proposition will best be realized with a package that

holds the greatest demographic and geographic appeal,

f.e., the soft pack.

With the exception of Marlboro and a limited number of
Stylish segment brands, box packaging has not proven to
be a highly leverageable asset. One might even be
tempted to define the box package ae something of a
Marlboro phenomenon.
