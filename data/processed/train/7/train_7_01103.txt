April 1, 1982

TO: R. A. Kampe

SUBJECT: Executional Direction Resulting from SALEM Spirit/SALEM
Slim Lights Qualitative Research

The attached MDD/Moderator reports summarize findings from the
SALEM Family creative focus groups conducted in Orlando, February 16
through February 19. The purpose of the groups was to qualitatively
evaluate executions for potential use as SALEM Family Spring/Summer
poolouts.

Based on consumer response to the SALEM Spirit and SALEM Siim
Lights comprehensives, the Brand's distinctive user/product
imagery appeared best communicated via visuals incorporating
the following elements:

@ SALEM Spirit

- Activities within the experience realm of the average younger
adult,

Visuals portraying activities that young adults can easily
relate to either from past experience or because the "tools"
for the activity are easily accessible (i.e., rubber tire,
raft), were widely accepted among respondents. Such activi-
ties appeared to capture the heart of SALEM Spirit as evi-
denced by one respondent's summation of the campaign as
“Good Times, Good Friends, Good Cigarette.

- Group participation versus individual competition.

Group activities (tubing, rafting, etc.) versus individually
competitive activities {motor bike racing, etc.) appeared
successful in communicating the sharing, warmth, and camaraderie
attributes essential to the SALEM Spirit campaign.

~ Executional story line.

Executions from which a clear story line can be drawn appeared
most impactful/meaningful to respondents. Both active and
passive executional settings were acceptable as long as story
lines were easily extracted.

- Refreshment imagery.

Environmental cues suggesting refreshment were found essential
for reinforcing the Brand's product imagery. Greenery, water,
and lighting were consistent communicators of this imagery among
focus group respondents.
