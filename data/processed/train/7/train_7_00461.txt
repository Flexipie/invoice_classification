SUBJECT: Aluminum Determination DATE: July 22, 1987
on Cigarette Condensate
Composite (A292, A293,
A297, A303, A304)

To: Ur. M. F. Borgerding PROM: R. T. White, Jr.

Cigarette Condensate samples were analyzed on the
Perkin-Elmer 5000 heated Graphite Atomizer-Atomio Absorption
Spectrophotometer (HGA-AA). A composite sample was prepared
by combining 5 samples (A292, A293, A297, A303, A304) of
cigarette condensate. Each of the 5 samples contained
condensate from 200 cigarettes collected by electrostatic
precipitation. A 2X dilution of the cigaratte condensate

composite sample was read against an aluminum calibration
curve.

The calibration curve (Figure 1) was prepared from
Certified Standard Reference Material 1412 (Multicomponent
Glass). ‘The powdered National Bureau of Standard material was
weighed into a solution of synthetic condensate and methanol
to form a slurry. The synthetic condensate, consisting of
glycerol:water (2:1), was prepared to match the matrix of
actual cigarette sample condensate. The statistical data from
the NBS - 1412 (slurry) curve can be seen in Table 1.
Analyses were made on duplicate samples prepared from the
eigarette condensate composite using the NBS-1412 calibration
curve. The statistical data can be seen in Tables 2 and 3.
