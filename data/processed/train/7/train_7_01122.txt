Subject: Weekly Highlights - Scientific Affairs
For Week of June 19, 1989

ITEMS FOR WEEKLY BRIEF

© Pesticides - Dr, Brian Smeeton recently returned from Greece,
where he learned that some farmers may be using the EBDC's as
"yellowing" agents. This use of the EBDC's is not in
accordance with good agricultural practice. Our brokers in
Greece are investigating this allegation, and famners will be
informed that residues of EBDC of this magnitude are not
acceptable. Regulatory action on EBDC's is expected from the
United States EPA in the next 3 to 6 months.

The Greek Tobacco Monopoly has recently banned the use of
endosulfan (thiodan) due to continued misuse by the farmer.
Acephate has been suggested as a replacement.

« Industrial Hygiene - Mr, Miller Jones issued a report to Dr.
R, Fletcher concerning a potential occupational dermatitis
case at Archer 200. Exposure to rolling oils was initially
thought te be the problem, but after investigation, Industrial
hygiene believes that the abrasive soap used for cleaning may
be the problem. we will await a decision from Dr. Fletcher
and the Medical Department before meeting with Packaging
management.
