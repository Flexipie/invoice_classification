Dear Bruce:

Thank you for your letter of December 20, which for
some reason, I didn't receive until the 28th,

In response to your question about what our strategy is
in considering Southland as package outlets and not
carton outlets, let me “plow” some old ground.

Prior to 1979, we placed carton fixtures in so-called
package sales type outiets. This included convenience
stores, liquor Stores, small volume food and drug out-
lets. We learned the hard way that we were paying
these retailers Shelf Display contract monies, pri-
marily, for storage of our product on our fixturing
Ratio of cartons versus package sales in these outlets
was approximately 90% by the package and 108 by the
carton.

These outlets, in addition to the traditional bona fide
carton sales outlets, represented an enormous budgetary BEST
cash outlay on 2 national basis. cory

We decided to correct this problem so we could improve
our Permanent Package Counter Display paid contract
program by identifying the various retail outlets as
Primarily package outlets or carton outlets. In taking
this action, we decided if 51% or more of the sales in
an outlet is by the package, it qualifies for our
Permanent Counter Display (which, by the way, pays more
than any other in our industry): and if 51% or more of
the sales is by the carton, it qualifies as a carton
outlet.
