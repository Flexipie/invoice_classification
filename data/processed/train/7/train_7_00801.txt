SUBJECT: Scientific Affairs Evaluation of XA Prototypes

You have requested Sclentific Affairs approval for consumer testing
of five XA prototypes. These prototypes utilize varying quantities of
magnesium and calcium acetate (10-22%), and 3% each of potassium acetate
and potassium chloride in the cuter wrap. The paper is 80% wood pulp and
20% CaCO2. The inner wrap is either wood pulp/tobacco (prototypes AB and
AC) or flax with varying amounts of calcium carbonate, magnesium hydroxide,
and potassium malate.

Ames data were generated for prototypes AB and AH {n a previous test
to establish limits for XA wrapper ingredients. No significant difference
fron a Camel Light control was noted in efther TA98 or TA10O activity.
Anes data were not requested for the remaining three prototypes since they
contain lesser amounts of the paper ingredients than prototypes AB and AN.

Prototypes AB and AH were submitted for a full battery of smoke
chemtstry analyses, whereas the other prototypes were analysed for only FIC
parameters, For AB and AH, most smoke chemisitry components were generally
decreased compared to a Camel Light contro}, with the exception of CO, HCN
and cresol (AH). HCN and cresal were within the range of existing market
brands, whereas the CO yield of prototype AB was slightly outside of the
market range:

xA Harket Range
co 13.9-38.7 4.9-18.6 mo/ctg
HEN 207-239 49-295 ug/etg
Cresols 6.5-7.7 2.4-29.3 ug/ety

These increases in CO, HEN and cresols were offset by large decreases in
some components such as acrolein and formaldehyde. For the same rationale
as Ames testing, the remaining prototypes were not submitted for the full
smoke chemistry battery.

Scientific Affairs recommends for the proposed consumer test. This
matter should now be referred to the HRRC for consideration.
