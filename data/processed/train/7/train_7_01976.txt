Dear Jim:

A few months ago in Rye Brook when Mac Cato and I showed you some of the
work we had done for Marlboro, you expressed some interesting thoughts about
the mega-brand concept and the eventual need for "a Marlboro for everyone.”

In light of the publicity glare surrounding RIR’s Uptown. we thought it best not
to focus on menthol right now, and in our analysis of untapped potentials, we
developed a line of thinking which we'd like to pass by you for comment before
proceeding to talk with The Brand,

It is spelled out on the attached, but in a nutshell the idea is that the brand's
powerful attraction to first time smokers yields a monster share of smokers up to
age 25, but then a steep decline cuts the share in half. Our proposal is grounded
in the belief that there may be "another Marlboro" latent in the original appeal
which, Holden Caulfield-style, might catch the fallout before it leaves the
franchise.

If this is @ long shot, it’s still worth a look because even a 10% success rate
would be equivalent to a serious brand. It's hard to imagine that any idea for
Marlboro hasn’t been looked at, and if this one is rejectable we'll appreciate
your having saved us the embarrassment, Meanwhile we'll turn over every stone,
‘willing to risk facing a boiling sea of maggots for a chance to find gold.

‘Yours truly,
