ROR INTEROFFICE MEMORANDUM

November 23, 1987

Mr. E, R, Hardman

Ms. A. G. Linville

Re: SALEM Light 85 Product Testing

SALEM Light 85 and SALEM Light 100 offer opportunities for
proactive resource management during ist Quarter 19388. The
issues at stake involve Manufacturing Confirmation, R&D
Confirmation, Competitive Wave Testing and Product Guidance
Testing. Specifics for each style will be discussed separately.

SALEM Light 85

Manufacturing Confirmation testing of CT-1244A is scheduled for
NFO mailout 1/4/88. R&D Confirmation is scheduled for NFO

mailout 2/8/88. The next available date for mailout of any

product will be 3/14/88 (based on a 5 week smoker turn around).
Competitive Wave I is also scheduled for 2/8/88. If the

prototype performance is confirmed in Manufacturing, the Wave I

test will be inactionable as the SALEM Light 85 product would

change. If however, the original prototype performance is not

confirmed, we will be faced with a choice: 1) mail the R&D

Confirmation test for use as a diagnostic tool in understanding

the prototype performance or 2) mail the Wave I Competitive read

of current product.

I do not believe that decision can be made at this tine, so I
Propose that we proceed with preparation of both products
(Competitive Wave I and the R&D Confirmation of CT-1244A) for the
2/8/88 NFO mailout. This approach will ensure that we are able
to effectively utilize those smokers as they become available.

Additionally, as discussed before, Wave I competitive smokers
will receive a cork tipped SALEM Light 95 while franchise smokers
will receive white tipped product.

Our previous plans to boost the first cork tipped wave to a full
sample will no longer be necessary as a result of the increase in
Andividual wave sample sizes. I Would like to discuss this point
with Cindi Munter and Kevin Verner as this represents a change
from our previous agreement.
