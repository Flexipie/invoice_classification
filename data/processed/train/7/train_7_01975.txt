RE: CAMEL's 1988 Direct Mail Program

This memo forwards the Brand's recommendation for CAMEL's 1988 direct mail
program.

Background

As currently scheduled, CAMEL will mail carton coupons to new compatitive
virile smokers in the PMSA in 1988, Of the $1,200M scheduled to be spent, :
$800M will be spent against smokers 30-34 to generate volume while $400M is

targeted for business building through mailings to 25-30 smokers.

Reconmendation
The Brand recoumends;

© Competitive virile suckers 21-34 in all CAMEL priority regions receive a
CAMEL offer. This would include those names currently scheduled to be
held for MAGNA's 3rd Quarter mailings.

e Rather than emphasizing volume generation, the program should focus on
converting young adult smokers by offering pack coupons that specifically
address the purchase patterns of this group.

® The creative should deliver the same overall image communicated through
the Brand's other promotional materials and, as such, should be developed
by the Brand's creative group under the Brand's direction.

Rationale

The above recommendation is based ou the following rational

# Rather then receiving WINSTON direct mail pieces, young adult smokers in
all CAMEL's priority regions should receive CAMEL offers:

= CAMEL, unlike WINSTON, possesses the image and personality that is
relevant to this group of target smokers.

= CAMEL continues to grow among young adult smokers in CAMEL's priority
regions while WINSTON does not, Moreover, in these priority regions,
CAMEL has a greater opportunity for conversion than any other RJR brand,
= CAMEL is designated as the number one priority younger adult brand.

~ Direct mail represents a vital and effective resource to use against
CAMEL's strategy of business building among young adult smokers.
