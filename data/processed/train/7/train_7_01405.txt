Cooled Air-Lock Rotor for G-19-1

October 6, 1992

This memo is to request your approval 10 implement use of water cooling in the Sublimator exit
air-lock for G-19-1 production.

This was origionally tested in connection with possible use alang with replacement of the
vibrating conveyors with belt conveyors, which is currenily on hold. This modification is now
ceded in order to reduce a problem DIET is experiencing with the exit air-lock seizing
whenever tobacco flow to the sublimator is interrupted, exposing the rotor to additional heat
causing it to expand and seize. After seizure occurs, production time is lost. while allowing it to
coal down, Another needed benefit from the cooled rotor would be a slight reduction in fumes.

G-19-1 product has been tested using the coaled air-lock rotor (TPD 1810) compared to a
control (TPD 1809) with both tests pulled from the same lot of strips. Both tests were processed
at the same conditions ( 20% m.c. infeed, 700°F sublimator, 5000 Ib/nr feedrate, 5000 b/hr
steam, standard reorder, etc.) Results summarize as follows:
