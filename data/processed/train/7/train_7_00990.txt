Dear Henry:
Thank you very auch for your letter of September 8 in reply

to mine of September 3 which, as you say, I had sent you
prior to my receipt of your letter of September 2.

In the last paragraph of your letter of the 8th you say

that, if you understand we correctly, I am “in effect stating
that the research program as such of C.T.R, should be pre-
sented to the Executive Committee of C.T.R. for approval,”
and that this deviates from your understanding “of the
Purpose of C.T.R. which is that C.T.R. should be independent
in formulating and effecting its research program."

I very much hope that any apparent difference of opinion
between you and me in this respect stems from the sense in
which each of us uses the words "research program." I would
say that it is entirely proper for the membership of C.T.R.
to determine its research direction, Thus, I think it
entirely proper and greatly to be desired that C,T.R. move
steadily from undirected or unprogranmed research, upon
grants approved by the SAB, to a determination by C.T.R.,
advised by the SAB, of those areas of research directly
related to tobacco where significant gaps in knowledge exist,
with appropriate research then coumissioned by C.T.R, That
is not to say that the member companies or the Executive
Committee should maintain, or even attempt, control of the
formulation or implementation of research to carry out the

research policy formulated by the members.
