RE: H.R, 4543
Dear Jims

I write this letter on behalf of the Freedom to Advertise
Coalition to express the views of the Coalition on the most
Fecent draft of Congressman Luken's bill, H.R. 4543. On June 6,
1gge, I sent you a copy of the testimony I gave before the
Subcommittee on H.R. 4543 on June 8. As I explained in ay
testimony, the Coalition is comprised of the American Association
of Advertising Agencies, the Amecican Advertising Federation, the
Association of National Advertisers, the Magazine Publishers
Association, and the Outdoor Advertising Association of
America. ‘The Coalition was formed out of concern for the
protection of the constitutional right to truthfully and
nondeceptively advertise legal products.

At the June 8 hearing, I testified that the Coalition
opposed enactment of the provision in H.R. 4543 that would
eliminate the existing system of exclusive federal control over
cigarette labeling and advertising on the issue of smoking and
health. 1 stated the Coalition's belief that deletion of the
federal preemption language from the Federal Cigarette Labeling
and Advertising Act would permit each individual state and
thousands of countiés, cities and other local municipalities to
require additional or different warnings than those required by
federal law. Such a provision would subject the advertising of a
legal, nationally-marketed product to a hodgepodge of state and
local requirements and restrictions which would severely limit,
if not end, the national advertising of cigarettes.
