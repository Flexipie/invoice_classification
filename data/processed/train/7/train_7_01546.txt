Dear Mr. Hobbs;

Since you are familiar with the work of our Director,
anitai Etzioni, we are holding for you an advance copy of
his forthcoming book An Inmodest Agenda (McGraw-Hill,
November 15, 1982}. Amitai Etzioni has been ranked as
the first among the country's thirty top analysts of public
policy in the period 1969 to 1980, In his new book, he

analyzes the next two decades in the U.S.A., both in terns

of what will happen and what might be done, On the personal
level he sees an end to hedonistic tendencies of the recent
past and a rise in commitment to serve others, not as an
altruistic act, but as part of a growing commitment of per-
sons to care for each other, as the government will serve
them less. Concerning the family, Etzioni expects great
investment in "parenting," through increases in concern of
both fathers and mothers for their children; more flexi-time,
less frequent reassignment of executives; and a re-stigmatiza-
tion of divorce where young children ave involved. Schools

will run from age 4 to 16, with the last two years combining
work and study. Fewer resources for colleges, more for
primary and secondary education. Above all, a renewed focus
on character formation, especially self-discipline, is the
needed quality, both for education and for adult life. On
