Dear Mr. Maxwell,

I am currently involved in a study that might be of
interest to your Corporation. As you know, the course of
cancer, and many other diseases as well, depends on the
balance of power betveen the invading forces of the
disease, the antigens, and the defense forces of the
organism, the antibodies. The human immune system, in
its genetic, biochemical and psychological aspects should
be able to defend the organism against any germs

viruses, or other destructive forces which may invade

The study of immunology is the search for an answer to
the question why it is or is not able to do so.

Undoubtedly, tobacco can cause harm to the
respiratory system and at present is the main cause of
lung cancer. However, not all heavy smokers develop
cancer and, in fact, only a fraction of smokers succumb
to lung cancer, which points to the possibility of
susceptability to cancer and respiratory disease as being
the culprit of a defective immune system. I am convinced
that current research shoulé@ be directed to the immune
system? that strengthening the defense forces of the
organism is the future course necessary in preventing
and/or saving millions of lives.

I vould greatly appreciate it if your staff coule
prepare for me an accurate report indicating:
1.) The number of heavy smokers.
2.) the annual number of people afflicted
with lung cancer.
3.) the annual mortality rate of heavy
smokers.
When I publish my study, due credit will be given to
your Corporation and staff members for their help and
