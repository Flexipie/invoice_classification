SUBJECT: MINNESOTA PACKAGING BILL - SF 95

According ta the summary which you have provided, SF 95 would have no
impact on RURT and it should be classified as either "acceptable" or,
at the very Teast, "neutral". It appears to be a reasonable solution
to the problem.

One of the most attractive features of the legislation is that it
imposes a broad-based "recycling" fee on residential homeowners,
apartment units and commercial industrial units. Any attempt to amend
Sections 7 or 8 to impose a “per package” tax would change our attitude
toward the bil? and make it “negative” for our purposes.

Also, the bill does not attempt to require that packaging materials be

degradable or recyclable. Any amendment to impose such packaging
requirements in Section 1 through 7 should also be opposed.
