Dear Dave:

Ignition testing has been completed for the second collaborative study
af the CORESTA Task Force On Cigarette Ignition Propensity. Sixteen
tests were run for each cigarette/fabric combination. The test results
are reported in the enclosed table.

The ignition results for each cigarette design showed a consistent trend
with increasing fabric salt concentration. However, the results that
showed Cigarette J to be mare ignition prone then Cigarette G were
opposite of expectations. It was observed that the ignitions of
Cigarette J on Fabric A4 all took place at the tipping paper/cigerette
paper interface.

Laboratory temperatures during the tests ranged from 74°F to 77°F dry
bulb and from 65°F ta 66°F wet bulb. The average weights of the
conditioned cigarettes (based on 200 cigarettes) were 619 milligrams
for Cigarette J and 982 milligrams for Cigarette G.
