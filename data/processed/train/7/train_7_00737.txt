DATE: April 12, 1989

SUBJECT: Status of Analytical Chemistry Support for Freon 123
Studies

A meeting was held April 10 to review the status of the chemistry
required’ to support the various studies being conducted on
tobacco expanded with Freon 123 (F-123). Those in attendance
were Henry Chung, James Elder, Brenda Hodge, Charles Rix, Scott
Appleton and Rhenda Steele. The following topics were discussed:

Pyrolysis of Freon 12

Henry Chung has pyrolyzed several samples of F-123 but
has not pyrolyzed a sample of the F-123 used to expand
the tobacco currently being tested. He agreed to
pyrolyze a sample of the current F-123. A projector
has been ordered that will pyrelyze samples and inject
pyrolysates directly into a gas chromatograph, giving a
more accurate analysis. This device will be here in
about one month.

Purity Characterization:

A purity characterization of the F-123 used to expand
tobacco in January has been conducted at RJRT. The
results were given to Rhenda Steele. Henry Chung keeps
all purity characterization memos on file.

Decay Characterization:

Residue data are available at the time of tobacco
expansion (January 21) and at the time cigarettes were
smoked (February 26) for the genetic toxicology
studies. Charles Rix is continuing a spot check
residue analysis every 10 days on these cigarettes.
