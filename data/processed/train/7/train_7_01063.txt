ni

Eighteen urine samples and seven standards were submitted by the
Pharmacology division for MDGC/MS analysis to validate methodology
for the analysis of nicotine and several nicotine metabolites

in urine. These metabolites include cotinine, 3'-oH-cotinine,
demethylcotinine and possibly nicotine-N-oxide. Standards of
denethylcotinine and nicotine-N-oxide were also submitted. These
standards were analyzed by GC/MS for confirmation, to determine

if they could be analyzed by GC and to determine ions to be monitored
for quantitation. Demethylcotinine was confirmed and chromatographed
well. However, nicotine-N-oxide apparently decomposes when introduced
into the GC via heated injection port. Several small studies were
perforned to confirm this phenomenon. These results could have
significant impact on the analysis of biological fluids for nicotine
where heated injection ports are used.

Smoke Components

n

A procedure for the determination of nicotine in saliva is presently
being performed by the Biobehavioral division. In addition to the
compounds of interest, several minor unknown compounds have been
detected. A request was submitted to determine if these components
were related to smoking. Samples of saliva collected before and
after smoking were analyzed by GC/MS. Only two compounds (butanoic
acid and 2-methyl-2-butenedioic acid) were found to be different in
the after smoking samples. The after smoking samples contained two
to three times more of these components than the samples taken prior
to smoking.
