TO: COMMUNITY RELATIONS COMMITTEES

Enclosed is a summary of recent éeve lopments on tobacco and
health and a report on the present status of lung cancer research.

You have undoubtedly read news reports of a study on tobacco
and its relation to health to be conducted by the U. S. Public
Health Service.

During preliminary planning, the Tobacco Institute and the
Tobacco Industry Research Comuittee were consulted by the Surgeon
General. He has announced that the study is to be comprehensive
and objective and will be concerned not only with tobacco but other
factors such as air pollution, viruses, etc..

Selection of members of the study group is expected to be
completed by mid-September, The first phase will consider the
nature and magnitude of the health hazard and is expected to
require about six months. ‘

This study is of major importance to the tobacco industry
and we will advise you promptiy of developments.

Meanwhile, may I urge you to send to the Institute news
clippings and other items relating to tobacco which come to your
attention. You will be performing a real service to the entire
industry by furnishing such information.

Cordial regards,

Ned Johnston :
August, 1962

The Tobacco Institute, Inc.

(Accompanying briefing papers prepared by Hill and Knowlton which
were furnished to members of the Public Relations Committee.)
