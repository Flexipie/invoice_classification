WHAT IS THE "TAR BLOCKER" CONCEPT ?

‘The "Tar Blocker" concept is centered around a change in the laser
perforations made in the cellulose acetate filter tow. This change is best
described as drilling deep and wide "slots" in the filter rather than the
conventional “holes” which are just through the papers and not into the tow
itself. The effect of deep “slots” is to introduce the air dilution air into the center
of the filter tow rather than at the periphery of the filter. This simple change has
the effect of forcing mainstream ‘tar’ to the outer edges of the filter tow rather
than concentrating it in the center of the filter. We believe that this change
effects the taste of the cigarette because the composition of the ‘tar’ is slightly
changed, even though the amount of ‘tar’ yielded from the cigarette is not
changed. We believe that ratios of some compounds in the ‘tar’ are changed and
thereby the taste is effected.

WHAT IS THE "TAR BLOCKER" THEORY ?

One current theory of why the ‘tar’ composition is changed is that a larger
cross sectional area of the filter is used for filtration, and for re-elution of trapped
‘tar’ from eatly puffs into the mainstream of later puffs. This could happen
because the concentration of ‘tar’ on the filter is a function of the area over which
that ‘tar’ is deposited. (Assuming the same ‘tar’ yield from similar tobacco rods,
in the case of the “Tar Blocker“ the material deposited will be less concentrated
and possibly more readily eluted back into mainstream smoke.)
