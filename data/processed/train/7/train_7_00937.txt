Dear Larry:

I appreciated the opportunity to visit the Danville Plant
last week and participate in the three meetings with your
selaried employees. You are to be commended for the excellent
way the meetings were set up and conducted. The large at-
tendance and the enthusiasm generated by those present was
a clear indication of the keen interest in the problems of
our industry.

It was a privilege to spend some time with you and Phil
Joseph and discuss the Tan Program prior to the meetings. It
was very nice of both of you to take me to lunch and I wel-
comed the opportunity to discuss some of the potential legisla-
tive problems in the tobacco region this year.

I was also extremely impressed by many of the questions
and comnents from your employees. It showed a good basic know-
ledge of the political system and a willingness to get involved
when the need arises. I am sure, as you predicted, that a very
large percentage of those present will become Tan enrolees.

Incidentally, the Danville Processing and Storage operation
is a beautiful facility and one of the most impressive in our
industry.

Again, many thanks for everything and I look forward to
working with you in the future.

Cordially,

Tee
