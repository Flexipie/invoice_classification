Dear Fellow -Employee:

As we approach the holiday season, we look back upon a year which
has been both challenging and rewarding for our company and our
industry, Everyone associated with R.J. Reynolds Tobacco Company
should take justifiable pride in the achievements our company has
recorded in 1982, for it has been a year in which teamwork
enabled us to maintain our growth and progress.

Certainly our company and industry faced a number of problems in
1983. Government-dictated changes in the tobacco price-support
system, proposals for new health warnings on cigarette packages
and ‘the doubling of the federal excise tax on cigarettes were
just some of the significant external influences on our business
which may have long-term effects.

But despite these problems, by almost every standard our company
became stronger. We broke ground for what will be the world's
largest and most-modern cigarette manufacturing facility. Our
product quality and productivity continued to significantly
improve. :

I want to thank you for the role you played with your fellow
employees in shaping this outstanding record. We will again face
challenges in 1983 and our best will be required to meet them.
But I an confident we can do it.

I hope that you and your family will enjoy a happy and safe
holiday season, and that you join me in believing that we all
have a great deal for which to be thankful as 1982 draws to a
close.

Sincerely,
