MEMORANDUM TO THE COMMITTEE OF COUNSEL

Re: OSHA Approval of California Hazard

Enclosed is a copy of the OSHA order. published last Friday,
approving the California Hazard Communication standard. OSHA ruled that the
California standard must pass the "product clause” test under the Federal OSH Act.
OSHA concluded, however, that the California standard, including Proposition 65
and its supplemental enforcement provision, satisfies the "product clause" test
because it (1) is at least as effective as the Federal Hazard Communication standard,
(2) does not pose an undue burden on interstate commerce and (3) is required by
compeliing local conditions. OSHA ruled that conditions unique to a state are not
necessary to establish compelling local conditions, that the burden of proof is on the
‘opponent of a state standard to show that the standard fails to meet the "product.
clause" test and that Proposition 65's supplemental enforcement provision (including
private right of action) does not render the California standard unapprovable. OSHA
alsa ruled that the California standard may not be enforced against out-of-state
manufacturers because a state OSHA plan may not regulate conduct occurring outside
