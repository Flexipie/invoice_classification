ITEMG FOR WEEKLY BRIEF:
Focus Group Exploratory on XGP Prototypes:

Several XGT products were presented to young adult smokers at
focus groups conducted in Chicago. Based on the comments of
these Marlboro and Marlboro Light smokers, the products were
perceived to be fairly smooth with a light smoke, not as strong
and satisfying as Marlbore and similar in taste to Marlboro
Lights or Benson and Hedges 100. Given the concept of a lower
tar product with similar delivery, the smokers showed an interest
in trying these prototypes as long as the taste was maintained.

Snoke_Constituents/Fate Studies:

A radiotracer study of C-14 labelled phenethyl isovalerate showed
that, unlike many volatile additives, this compound remained
primarily in the tobacco rod after aging 8 weeks (65%/35%;
rod/filter), Delivery to the mainstream TPM (14% of the total)
is similar to additives which show preferential migration to the
filter, This data will be compared to fate studies previously
completed on other flavor chemicals to determine whether vapor
pressure is the only driving force for flavor migration in a
cigarette.

ITEMS OF R&D INTEREST!
Ignition Propensity:

Ignition tests were completed for six prototypes provided by
Kimberly-Clark. ‘Two blend types (full flavor and 50% expanded
tobacco) were used with each of three wrapper designs (low
permeability/high citrate, high citrate bands and paper bands).
These prototypes were designed to sustain smolder in air but
self-extinguish when in contact with a substrate material.

During the initial tests, the prototypes self-extinguished on the
furniture mockups. Mockup ignitions were observed, but usually
only after re-lighting cigarettes which had extinguished.
