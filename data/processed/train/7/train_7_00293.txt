Dear Mr. Arledge:

The promos for the November 22 ABC News 20/20
segment on environmental tobacco smoke (“ETS”) promised
viewers the “truth about passive smoking.” We participated in
the segment because we were told the piece would be balanced
and fully investigated. The story, however, was neither
balanced nor fully investigated. Clearly, drama and conflict
drive television today, and both were delivered.
Unfortunately, the truth was lost somewhere along the way.

take issue with several aspects of the segment, but I am
most disappointed that 20/20 ignored the opportunity to
investigate and focus on the feal story — the actual scientific
studies regarding ETS — not on inflammatory sound bites, not
fon our company’s position on active smoking, not on 35 year-
old digarette ads, but on the science regarding ETS.

There was one tiny, throw-away piece of narration:
“There is confusion over the science. Many studies the EPA
looked at showed no significant increased risk of lung cancer.”
