NICOTINE IN CELLULOSE ACETATE FILTERS

I, INTRODUCTION

Nicotine retained in cellulose acetate filters after smoking
is quantitatively determined in celluiose acetate filters by gas
chromatography of a methanol extract using a flame ionization
detector. Carvone is used as the internal standard to minimize
errors caused by instrumental variation.

Nicotine is calculated by comparing the ratio of peak area
of nicotine to carvone. Results are recorded as mg/filter.

For a typical commercial sample, the relative standard
deviation at the two-signa level is +15%. Results are reported
in mg/filter. .

Nicotine and water collected on the TPM pads during smoking

of this sample can be used to quantitate FTC tar.

II. SAMPLE REQUIREMENTS

Sample Recommended: 20 filters (split)

Sample per Determination: 2 split filters
Replicate Determinations Normally Made: 10
Man-hours per Determination: 2 hours

Man-hours for Replicate: 3 hours/10 determinations

Total Elapsed Time: 3 hours/10 determinations
