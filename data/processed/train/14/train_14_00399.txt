I. Microwave Motsture Meter

A test of the microwave moisture meter on the MK IX maker at the
Training Center also produced poor correlations between microwave readings
and OV. A follow-up test was then run on the R&D integrated MK VIII maker
which has a variable speed drive to determine if making speed affected the
microwave readings. The results indicated that as speed increased (1000,
2000 and 3000 cigarettes/min) the microwave power absorbed increased. How-
ever, the microwave readings correlated poorly to OV at all three speeds,

while testing a microwave circulator for temperature stability it
was observed that the microwave oscillator was quite sensitive to temper-
ature changes. A 9°C temperature change caused the oscillator output
to change 0.35mw (approximately 4% power absorbed) while the circulator
transmission changed only 0.03mw. Tests are planned to determine if this
temperature effect could explain the poor performance of the moisture meter.
