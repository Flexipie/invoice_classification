Tobacco Extrusion
Objective

To develop a process for extrusion of foamed tobacco articles.

Status:

Modifications to the post-extrusion line were successfully completed .
The complete line was run several times with smooth operation at 2,000
wrapped rods per minute. Studies are underway in an effort to
increase the line's capability to cool the extrudate prior to wrapping
in order to increase flexibility for product devetopment.

Plans:
Begin product development using the completed continuous line.
Efforts to reduce the QTD of the wrapped rod are of primary

fmportance. Installation of a flavor addition system 1s also
underway.

Foamed Filler Binder

Objectiv

To develop a process for applying a subjectively acceptable foamed
binder to the tobacco filler in order to mprove coal strength, reduce
Joose ends and allow for weight reduction.

Status:

Production scale test runs using the foam applicator to apply binders
to the cigarette at the maker were successfully completed at FTR's
factory in Neuchatel, Switzerland. A Mark 9 maker at 5,000 cigarettes
per minute linked to’a GO packer was used for the evaluation.
Approximately 3-4 mg licorice and Q.1 mg pectin per cigarette were
‘applied to both Marlboro and Stanton prototypes. Preliminary results
from quality index evaluations as well as subjective screening by
their smoking panel appear promising for the models with 15 to 30 mg
weight reductions.

Plan:

Continue evaluation of binder systems using thermally degraded citrus
pectin as well as pectin extracted fron burley stems.
