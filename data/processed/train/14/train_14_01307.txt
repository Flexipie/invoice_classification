Aniline Spill

A representative of the Analytical Research Group visited PH
Silvertown after an aniline spill by a neighbouring
manufacturer near the PM site. Samples were taken of
materials and finished products which could have been
contaminated by aniline vapor (MUN), Analysis by static
headspace gas chromatography demonstrated that no
contamination by aniline had occurred (FMO}, Te was
recommended that materials helé pending investigation be
used for production (1) (NUM).

Cigarette Spotting

The cause of a Raffles cigarette paper spotting problem
which occurred at PM Silvertown has been investigated, On
the basis of TLC and IR spectroscopy it was concluded that
the most probable cause of the problem was a leak of
compressor oil into the compressed air lines (2) (MUM).

Rothmans Cigarettes

An investigation into the cause of contamination of a sample
of Rothmans cigarettes was requested (3). Investigations by
IR spectroscopy and capillary GC after saponification
indicated that the contamination was caused by contact with
cooking oil of animal or vegetable origin (4) (YVG/MUM) -

Strings in Swiss Tobacco

The analysis of some strings found on Swiss tobacco was
requested by Leaf Department FTR (5}. Investigation by I
spectroscopy, elemental analysis and static headspace GC’
indicated that the material was polypropylene (6) (MUM/FNO) .
