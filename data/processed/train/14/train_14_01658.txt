I. INTRODUCTION AND SUMMARY

Following the plans setforth in the last months report,
we concentrated on attaining our goal of a 14 mg/cigt. TPM
delivery, we completed the series of experiments in progress
on paraffin treated fillers; and we continued our efforts
towards increasing the flavor of low delivery cigarettes. In
addition to these activities, the Piller Modification group
began work on means of raising the nicotine delivery of the
current low TPM cigarette,

Our efforts this month produced the following results:

1. A cigarette delivering approximately 14 mg/cigarette
of TPM at an average RTD of 5.4" was evaluated.

2, The data on paraffin treated (1.1% paraffin) Parlia-
ment filler show a small decrease in TPM ‘when con-
pared with untreated filler) with no incraase in RID
during the smoking process. Previous resuits showed
an increase in RTD during smoking at a paraffin level
of 1,8-2.08 in the filler. The effect of tne 1.1%
level of paraffin was not discernable by a taste panel.

« The Filler Modification group has found a method
which may be effective for increasing the nicotine
delivery of low TPM delivery cigarettes.

II. EXPERIMENTAL PROCEDURES, RESULTS, AND DISCUSSTON
A. Low Delivery Cigarettes

A series of four experimental models was evaluated during
this period as part of our effort to produce a cigarette with
a delivery of 14 mg of TPM. All samples contained Parliament
filler which was encased in the Ecusta TOD 899 cigarette
paper (monophosphate treated, 16-25 seconds} previously dis-
eussed. This paper gives a filler rod dilution of about 11%.
