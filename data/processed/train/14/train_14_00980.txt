I, BASELINE STUDIES

Puff-by-puff TPM delivery data was obtained on the rods
made to ten different circumferences. This gave approximately
70 data points on TPM delivery as a function of circumference
and puff number. A technique was developed for correlating
all of this daca wich mathamacical models. We found that the
TPM data could be fitted with a high degrée of reproducibility
to a polynomial formula with six terms. The slope and shape of
puff-by-puff profile was the same for all circumferences. The
computer programs for the mathematical analysis were made very
general so, that they can be applied to any type of, puff-by-
puff data.* We plan firs co extend the work to nicotine and
water data from the circumference baseline study,
