The selectivity of cigarette filter materials for specific

components of smoke has long been recognized. The references in

at this time and thus I shall only mention some of the representative
examples. :

activated carbon reduced selectively the concentration of certain
tts gases in smoke. Later, Hoffman and Wynder (3), and

Anvestigeted factors that affect the selective filtration of nico-
tine. Lakritz, Stedman and Strange (5) last year reported the .
effect of smoke pH on the filtration of various smoke components.

Along with investigating the phenomenon of selectivity several
authors have proposed expressions to describe the degree of selective
filtration.
