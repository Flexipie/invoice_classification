OBJECTIVE

Develop and evaluate the methodology for the single cigarette
routine determination of sidestream smoke yields.

STATUS

Method development.

An additional single-line system for the collection of sidestream
smoke (SS) was set up, identical in its design to the existing
four-channel equipment [1], In this apparatus, the top of the
collector chimney was fitted with a Cambridge filter, but the
back-up trap for nicotine sampling was omitted.

The SS gas phase exhausted through the Cambridge filter vas
analysed using a TESTA 123 flame ionisation detector (FID).

A study was performed to assess the possibility of estimating the
cigarette deliveries of total gas phase organic compounds (TG0}
in the $s using this set-up.

> Cigarettes
All experiments were performed with the IMI3 monitor cigarette
with no selection by weight or draw resistance.

- Ruggedness
The distinction between “particulate matter" and "gas phase
organic compound” is rather arbitrary, The partition of the smoke
components between the 2 phases is determined by the combination
of the type of filtering system, temperature, pressure and gas
velocity through the filter. Therefore, the measurement of the
TG0 delivery will depend on these experimental parameters.

To estimate the magnitude of this effect, the flow rate and the
type of filtering system of the provisional reference procedure
(use of one 9~cm Cambridge filter with a 5 l/min air flow) were
altered. The variations brought about by these changes in the
operating conditions are summarized in Tables 1+2.
