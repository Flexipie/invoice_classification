OBJECTIVE

Develop and evaluate the methodology for the single cigarette
routine determination of sidestream smoke yields.

STATUS

Method development

An additional single-line system for the collection of sidestream
stoke (SS) was set up, identical in its design to the existing
four-channel equipment [1}. In this apparatus, the top of the
collector chimey was fitted with a Cambridge filter, but the
back-up trap for nicotine sampling was omitted.

The SS gas phase exhausted through the Cambridge filter was
analysed using a TESTA 123 flame ionisation detector (FID).

A study was performed to assess the possibility of estimating the
cigarette deliveries of total gas phase organic compounds (T¢O)
in the S$ using this set-up.

organic compound" is rather arbitrary. The partition of the smoke
components between the 2 phases is determined by the combination
of the type of filtering system, temperature, pressure and gas
velocity through the filter. Therefore, the measurement of the

To estimate the magnitude of this effect, the flow rate and the
