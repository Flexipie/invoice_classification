ABSTRACT

The structures of twenty-five alkyi substituted pyridines and their cor=
responding Nenethylpyridiniun cations were calculated using semi-empirical all-
valence electron (MINOO/3) self-consistent-field procedures. The effects of sub-
stituents on the ring systems were exawined with particular attention focused on
the changes on the aromatic ring bond angles. The energy of methylation for these
twenty-five pyridines was calculated by subtracting the total energy of each
pyridine free base from the total energy of the carresponding N-metnylpyridiniun
cation. An excellent correlation was obtained between this calculated energy of
methylation and Srown's experimental heats of trifluoroboronations for the same
pyridines; implications of this correlation are discussed. Non-additive struc-

tural and energetic effects are calculated and evaluated.
