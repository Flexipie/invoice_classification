Ingredient Transfer into Mainstream Smoke

Abstract

Published literature is reviewed for information on transfer of ingredients from filler into
mainstream smoke (MS). Information is available on pesticides, herbicides, model
flavorants, carbohydrates, hydrocarbons, humectants, benzo(a)pyrene, metals. and
nicotine, Smoking was dene mainly under FTC conditions. Ingredient transfer appears to
follow a general pattem of about 10-30 % transfer to MS, 30-40 % transfer into sideatream
smoke (SS), and the rest remaining in the butt. Volatility of ingredients determines the ratio
of parent compounds to pyrolytic products, and the distribution in MS and SS. The
dependence of the MS yield on the distribution of ingredients In the filler, paper, and filter
is complex. In view of nonzero MS transfer rates for compounds with higher molecular
weight mechanisms other than volatization and including some type of mechanical carry
‘over must contribute to the transfer.

Introduction

Alliterature search on ingredient transfer into cigarette MS was conducted and the
keywords used and the literature found in the search are presented.

Various groups of compounds were investigated. Because MS yields are dependent on
the way cigarettes are smoked. ihe smoking procedure is discussed. Sampling of the
various smoke fractions is described and analytical methods used to measure specific
compounds are addressed. MS yields of compounds are given together with features of
potential influence and a generalization of the patterns visible in the individual result is
made.
