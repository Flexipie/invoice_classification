Vapor phase nicotine was determined in 17 randomly selected
supermarkets having no smoking restrictions in order to estimate
exposures of occupants to environmental tobacco smoke (BTS).
Personal samples were collected unobtrusively for approxinately :
one hour with adsorption tubes containing XAD-4 resin. Constant
flow sampling pumps provided an airflow of one liter per minute-

Collected nicotine was desorbed with ethylacetate and analyzed by
gas chromatography with nitrogen specific detection. The limit
of detection was 0.02 ug nicotine corresponding to 0.3 ug/m73 for
a 60 liter air sample. Vapor phase nicotine concentrations
ranged from < 0.3 to 3,3 ug/m73 with a geometric mean of 0.7
ug/ms. , These low nicotine concentrations are consistent with the
limited amount of smoking observed in these environments.
