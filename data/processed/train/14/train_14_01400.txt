Z,  EMPREGNATION STUDIES

A, Water Impregnation

Research on the use of surfactants and on the mechanism
of the water expansion process are in progress to help improve
water penetration and swelling characteristics of tobacco to
achieve expansion at economically attractive water impregnation
levels, Selective extraction of tobacco has proven that Lipids
and waxes hinder the rate of water entry and swelling of fibers.
It has been shown that small concentrations of surfactants
(0.01% level) are effective in inducing swelling. Efforts to
find a surfactant which cen interact in a vapor phase and with
winimun detrimental health considerations have led to the use of
ectyl aleohol vapors, Preliminary results are promising and
pilot acale testing is planned.
