OBJECTIVE(S) AND METHODOLOGY: The object of this project is to investigate
the possible enhancement of diet-induced atherogenesis by components of
cigarette smoke. :

Groups of beagle dogs are-being exposed to smoke from cigarettes containing
high and low levels of nicotine. In some of the animals, the smoke bolus

is augmented with carbon monoxide to produce further elevation of ‘
carboxyhemoglobin Tevels. Periodic hematologic, bload lipid and smoke
dosimetric measurements will be performed for subsequent correlation with
gross anatomical microscopic and tissue lipid assays to determine if
Nicotine and carbon monoxide are synergistic with diet induced atherogenesis.

FINDINGS: The dogs have been on the study for only six months, so that results
to date are limited. Dogs receiving cigarette smoke alone are achieving
carboxyhemoglobin levels of 13 to 14% saturation, while those receiving
augmented carbon monoxide doses and carbon monoxide alone have carboxyhemagTabin
Jevels in the range of 20 to 25% saturation. ‘The Ehrhardt atherogenic diet
being used for all dogs has increased plasma cholesterol to a level of 800 -
000 mg after 13 weeks exposure. Data are not yet available from the six
month sacrifices, although one dog which died spontaneousty shortly before

the scheduled sacrifice did exhibit extensive aortic plaque formation.
