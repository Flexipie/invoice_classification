COMBUSTION MECHANISMS (Waymack, Kang)

Plans are under way to examine at different heating rates a variety of
pre-formed chars (prepared at different temperatures) to explore mechanisms
of PAH formation, Chars with different degrees of aromatization will also
be examined, Interestingly in this regard, lignin heated at 100°/sec
Produces more char than lignin heated at 20°/min. In addition, wood lignins
Produce more char than Klason lignins at the same heating rates,

Thermal diffusivity measurements are being made on extruded rods which
have been dried and then rehydrated with known amounts of water.
Comparisons are being made to control Moniter cigarettes.

Pyrolysis experiments with different tobacce before and after
Supercritical extraction with CO, have. been initiated, Earlier
determinations of the extracts from these tabaccos revealed CO, extraction
of alxaloids,

HEAT CAPACITY OF TOBACCO (Tiller)

Work continues on the heat capacity of bright tobacco as a function of
temperature and moisture. Samples have been prepared containing OV
moistures of 3.3, 4.8, 5.8, 8.1, 9.0, 13.4, 19.0, 21.8, 24.7, 26.2 and
32.9%, Hopefully, from the quenching experiments a determination of the
glass transition temperature as a function of moisture can be obtained.
