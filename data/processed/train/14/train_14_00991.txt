I. EXPANSION PROCESSES
A, Water Expanded Tobacco

SEF booth testing of cigarettes made at a 30% level of
WET (replacing bright) showed them equally preferred to Marlboro
and a modified Marlboro with 25% ET, Test cigarettes with 15%
WET were judged milder than either of the controls.

A preliminary economic evaluation of the WET process
has been prepared and issued by F, Friedman,

The Jetstream has been repaired and is now operational.
Tests to define the "standard curves" for expansion of both
vacuum impregnated and ambient impregnated materials are plauned.

Tests using co~impregnants to achieve improved product
expansion at reduced (and therefore more economical) water
4mpregnation ratios have been initiated, Preliminary pilot
evaluation tests of a continuous impregnation process using
solutions of NH3/CO2 salts is also underway,

B. COg Process Development ~ Phase II

A study was made to relate the CV of expanded bright
with the final blended CV as a function of addition level. The
results showed that the increase in CV of the blend was directly
proportional to the amount and GV of the expanded portion,

DIRT expansion of Turkish tobacco was successfully
demonstrated, Percentage gain in CV was similar to that of bright.

The reimpregnation study (reimpregnation of degassed
impregnated bright filler) was continued for six cycles, The
results showed a loss of 18% propylene glycol in the impregnated
state after six impregnation cycles, No other major chemical
change waa noted, ‘There was no significant physical or chemical
change in the expanded product.
