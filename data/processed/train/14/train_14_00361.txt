I, Introduction and Susmary

The nineteen samples of cigarettes made from formulated,
(see note) cast, sheets were subjectively smoked by a panel
of five persons. The cigarettes were presented in pairs, and
the panel was asked in each case to rate the "test" cigarette
against 2 control having a constant composition, A sunmary
of the findings (Table I) shows that judgements were made
primarily on the basis of harshness. “The cigarettes were
unflavored and (on account of their 100% stem composition)
had a mainly djectionable sidestream smoke quality. There
were mo unanimous (5:0) judgements. The most consistant
“bestiworst" choices were expressed in reference to cigarettes
which contained high and no citrate; no and high-potassium,
Altho the decision was clear with respect to citrate, the
decisions were divided with respect to the other di-basic
acid (malic) which is normally present in far greater amounts
Although the panel disliked cigarettes having very high
potassium content, and liked a cigarette having no potassium,
three of the preferred cigarettes had a normal potassium
content.

The soluble fire-retardant ingredient used on a paper
supplied by Celanese as a smoking material has been tentatively
identified’ as a mixture of magnesium and ammonium sulfates.
