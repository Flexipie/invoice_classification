Vol. 19, No. 14 April 22, 1997

RJR NABISCO REPORTS 1997 FIRST QUARTER RESULTS
First Quarter Highlights

Net income per share up 9% to $.62
Net income up 8% to $213 million

Operating company contribution up 2% to $811 million
Cash net income up 4% 10 $340 miltion

RIR Nabisco Holdings Comp. (NYSE:RN) (oday announced resulls for the first quarter
ended March 31, 1997, Net income per common share was $.62 for the quarter, up

9 percent from net income per common share of $.57 reported in the first quarter of 1996,
Net income for the quarter was $213 million, up 8 percent from net income of

$198 miltion in the first quarter of 1996,

Operating company contribution (operating income before amortization of trademarks
and goodwill) for the first quarter of 1997 was $811 million, up 2 percent from

$798 million for the first quarter of 1996, Net sales for the 1997 first quarter were

$3.78 billion, a 3 percent decrease compared to net sales of $3.89 billion in the same
quarler last year.

“We delivered solid overall earnings growth for the quarter,” said Steven F. Goldstone,
chairman and chief executive officer of RIR Nabisco. "However, our operating profit was
up only slightly from the prior year. Overall sales were soft primarily as a result of
temporary export disroptions in the former Soviet Union in the tobacco business and the
short-term impact of a sales force reorganization in the U.S. Biscuit business which
caused some delivery shortfalls during the tansition period. We have already put
programs in place to remedy the tobacco volume sofiness and we rentain confident that
1997 will be a very good year in terms of earnings growth and business strengthening,”

Cash net income (net income before after-tax amortization of trademarks and goodwill)
for the quarter was $340 million, up 4 percent from $326 million in 1996.

‘The company also noted that 1996 first-quarter results included a one-time pre-tax charge
in the quarter of $11 million or $.02 per share associated with a proxy contest last year.
