Tuesday
April 11, 1995

Lawmakers in MARYLAND adjourned last night...Governor signed bill,
41368, that exempts establishments with liquor licenses from Dept
of Licensing  Regulation's workplace smoking ban, Defeated other
tobacco-related bills including S171, to prohibit "spiking" of
cigarettes...$301, to increase cigarette tax by 4 cents,..S621, to
impose 25% tax on smokeless tobacco...S16, to restrict vending
machines...H58, to prohibit smoking in vehicles when minors are
present...H1044, ADAMHA bill that increases penalties...H999, to
increase penalties for sale to minors...H122, to provide
initiative and referendum authority

NORTH DAKOTA legislature adjourned 4/7...82354 was signed by
Governor...as introduced, would require records on contributors to
initiative and xeferendum efforts. Defeated other tobacco-related
bills...$2498 would provide ADAMHA enforcement and preempt local
restrictions.,.H1316 would provide ADAMHA enforcement and
authorize local restrictions on sale of tobacco...H1367 would ban
smoking in child services facilities, and require designated areas
to be enclosed...82486 and 52385 would earmark tobacco tax
revenues.

ADAMHA/preemption bill in INDIANA passed House today by 60-38 vote
-..returns to Senate for concurrence.,.as amended in House, $157
would place ADAMHA reporting with Dept of Mental Health and
compliance testing with state/local law enforcement officials...
would prohibit use of minors for compliance testing...would
authorize only the general assembly to regulate sale, distribution
and display of tobacco products.

Smoking bill in SOUTH CAROLIWA has been amended in subemte to
Prohibit smoking in schools...H3201 also would allow designated
smoking areas in publicly-owned buildings, rather than require
them ...would prohibit distribution of tobacco products to
minors... would place enforcement responsibilities with Dept of
Revenue & Taxation...would preempt local restrictions on sale,
use, display, distribution and promotion of tobacco
products..,awaits action in House Agriculture cmte.

WEN RAMPSAIRE budget bill, #2, was amended in House Finance Cmte
to repeal cigarette tax stamp requirements...floor consideration
is expected on 4/13.

New bills in MASSACHUSETTS...4165@ would ban vending machines and
