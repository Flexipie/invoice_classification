March 13, 1997

To: Jan Krebs

From: Henry Stokes

Subject Weekly Status - Region III
EXCISE TAXES.

© The 4-cent (21-25-cents) tax bill in NEW MEXICO will be heard in House
committees this week. The bill has passed the Senate and one of the Co-
sponsors is the President of the Senate, Funding would go to the Cancer
Research Center at the University of New Mexico, The bill has a tiple referral
and a grassroots effort is underway.

ADAMHA

* The House in ARIZONA rejected the industry's ADAMHA bill by one vote, 27-
26, with three representatives taking a walk and four absent. We will attempt
an amendment in the Senate.

LCeEr

* In NEW MEXICO, a bill that would require licensing of tobacco retailers,
passed Senate Judiciary Committee after anti-preemption provisions were
removed and the preemption language contained in the current state law was
inserted (distribution and age). Bill was then referred to the second of four
committees.

PREEMPTION

Abill in ARKANSAS, which started out to be the industry preemption bill
carried by Representative James Dietz, has taken a different shape.
Preemption has been stripped out and Dietz has been killed in the press. The
Health Department has amended the bill and Distz is committed to passing
the bill on the House floor if for nothing else than to “save face". We will slop
the bill in the Senate.
