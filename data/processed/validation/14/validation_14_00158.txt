1, COAL STRENGTH STUDIES

A. Cigarette Circumference

Cigarettes with target circumferences of 22.0, 23.0, 24.0
and 25.0 mm have been made and are being circumference selected
to #0.1 mm of the target prior to coal strength testing. The
papers used to make the cigarettes were slit to the proper widths
to maintain a constant 2 mm lap for each circumference. The data
obtained from this test will be analyzed to see how weil it fits
the formula for the bending modulus of a right circular cylinder
referenced by C. Hoelzel in a memo on coal strength dated May 10,
1977,

B. Cigarette Paper Studies

The circumference test described above was made using two
different porosity papers (16 vs. 30 second) in order to collect
additional data on the effect of paper porosity on coal strength.
Additional tests have been planned to further investigate the
relationship between cigarette paper and coal strength, These
tests will be conducted as soon as the experimental papers can be
provided by the Paper § Filler Modification group.

C. Coal Strength Test Variation

During the past month, the coal strength monitor data were
recorded according to operator to test for operator-to-operator
variation. The results showed no significant differences in the
data obtained by the three primary operators.

II. Moisturized Carbon Studies

Parliament 100 cigarettes made with standard carbon vs.
moisturized carbon in the filter have been undergoing cold storage
and shelf aging studies for four months. The gas phase reduction
data show no differences between the standard carbon and the
moisturized carbon samples after the four months of aging. The
peculiar fact about this aging study is that the filler 0.V. levels
(especially in the case of the standard carbon cigarettes} are not
decreasing with age. The decrease in filler 0.V. in production
Parliament 100 cigarettes was the reason for investigating the use

of moisturized carbon.
