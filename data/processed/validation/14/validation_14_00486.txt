A. Qbjective: To develop a process for applying a subjectively acceptable
foamed binder ta the tobacco filler during making to improve cigarette
quality.

B. Resulte: Firmess rasults from Cambridge Lights cigarettes made with 1¢

pectin binder added to the rod at the maker show 9 4.1-0.15 mm pack
fimmess improvement over the control at standard weight. The results
also show that at this binder addition level, pack firmness numbers
equivalent to those of tha control are achieved with =24 ag less
tobacco. In order to achieve these pack firmness results, the OV of the
feed tobacco was reduced by 1.58 OV units to compensate for the
moisture increase resulting from the binder addition. Hewever, with the
Pectin costing approximately $11.26/1b, the above results do not appear
to be cost effective at this high binder addition rate, As a result,
development efforts to radesign the binder and nozzle applicator systers
are in progress to maximize the firmess improvement at the lower
addition rates (les than 0.3% add-on). Evaluation of a nozzle tip

with a reduced diameter (from 0.063" i.d. to 0.047" i.d.) looks very
Promising. Low foam densities (less than 0.15 g/cc) were achieved
without excessive pressure build-up in the foam generator. This
Pressure was minimized by telescoping down to the reduced diameter at
the final half inch of the nozzle. Testing of nozzles with diameters
raduced further is planned. objective is to further increase the foam
velocity to match or slightly exceed the suction tape speed thus
increasing the dispersion of binder within the rod.
