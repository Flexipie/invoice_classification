The equations describing combustion processes and flow processes
in burning cigarettes are now being expanded to include the
solid phase plus several species. in order to do this kinetic
expressions describing tobacco vaporization, condensation
processes, and carbon oxidation would be convenient. An

initial investigation of several tobacco weight loss curves
indicates that the gross kinetics can be described by a sum

of simple “one-step” first order kinetic functions. Presently,
the carbon oxidation and condensation data are being investiga
ted to see if a similar simplification can be made.

A set of experiments were completed that were designed to
measure dilution effects in cigarettes with conditions that
simulate a burning cigarette at high flow rates. In general,
the dilution through the paper increases with increasing flow
for Marlboro type cigarettes. However, for cigarettes with
dilution holes in the filter the total dilution decreases
slightly with increasing flow rate. The experiments will be
described in detail in a separate report.

Work has continued on the oxidation of thin foils of Ta to be
used as substrates for deposition of thin-film thermocouples.
It is possible to obtain a continuous and uniform thin film of
oxide which has a very high electrical resistance. The first
Au/Ni thin film thermocouples were deposited and calibrated
against 43 mil diameter wire thermocouple of the same
materials between room temperature and 100°C. The wire
