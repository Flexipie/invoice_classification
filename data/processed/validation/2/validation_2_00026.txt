RJ. Reynolds Tobacco Company
Statement on National Household Survey on Drug Abuse

August 21, 1996

We were not at all pleased to hear that the latest survey by the National Household
Survey on Drug Abuse (NHSDA) showed large percentage increases in 1995 among 12-
to 17-year-olds in usage of illicit drugs, marijuana, hashish and inhalants. We were
encouraged by the fact that the youth smoking rate -- whether by experimentation or
regular

smoking -- did not increase, but held constant,

We agree with Department of Health and Human Services Secretary Donna B. Shalala
and White House drvg ezar Barry McCaffrey when they say that education - by parents
and schools -- is the best solution to reduce youth experimentation with illicit drugs,
alcohol or tobacco. The increase in the rate of illicit drug use is alarming, and clearly
indicates that while the Clinton Administration has made the drug issue a top priority,
more emphasis needs to be placed in that area instead of on the Administration's proposal
to place increased regulation on tobacco -- a legal product used by 48 million adults.

Research has long indicated that peer influence, parental example and access to cigarenies
are the reasons kids begin smoking, and the Administration's proposal outlined by the
Federal Drug Administration will have a negligible effect on the youth smoking rate.

‘The tobacco industry for years has addressed these concerns with a measure of success,
Since 1991, R.J. Reynolds Tobacco bas offered and/or supported a variety of programs
aimed at curbing youth smoking. We have offered to parents brochures on how to talk to
their kids about not smoking, offered schools 4 curriculum program on handling peer
pressure to engage in inappropriate behaviors, and offered retailers a training program to
help sales personnel identify and decline sales to underage buyers of cigarettes,

‘These materials can be made available by calling 1-800-366-8441.
