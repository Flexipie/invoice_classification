Georgia -- Jeft Draughn, Farmer's Tobacco Warehouse, Claxton, GA, reported that on
12/21, growers at his warehouse wrote 38 letters to Senator Nunn, Congressman
Kingston and the FDA. Draughn also got S6 more letters written to Nunn, Kingstan and
the FDA trom three other warehouses.

Florida -- SR leader Shirley Deily and activist Dennis Quinn got letters published this
week in the St, Petersburg Times on the FDA issue. “Don't turn kids into pawns” was
the headline on the letters as Shirley and Dennis blasted the anti-smoking groups for
getting school kids to write letters opposing the FDA regulations. Both letters were in
response to a story in the paper detailing letters that were sent in to the FDA.

Jowa -- Ally Milder recruited 20 letters to the editor on the FDA issue from lowa SR
leaders and coalition partners. The letters were sent to the Des Moines Register, the
Waterloo Courier, the Sioux City Journal, and the Cedar Rapids Gazette.

Tennessee ~ State SR President Phil Pardue has been asked by Allanta’s Cohen &
Wolfe PR firm to speak out against FDA regulation of tobacco. Phil will be speaking
regarding the issue before civic groups in Memphis and Chattanooga in upcoming
weeks,

Indiana -- SR activist Arie Reynolds has collected another 1,000 FOA names on
petitions over the Christmas holidays at northwest Indiana bingo halls, bowling alleys
and taverns. This is in addition to the 1,300 he collected earlier in 1995. The Indiana
SR group is working out a plan to get them to Sens. Coats and Lugar and Rep.
Viscloskey when appropriate.

Florida -- Ken Walton participated in a T\ conference call this week and was asked to

Bert Gomez of Corporate Affairs to gather information about several south Florida
