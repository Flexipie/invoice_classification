Functional relationship data established in the 1999 Massachusetts Benchmark Study can be
applied to estimate smoke constituent yields for other cigarettes found in the U.S. market
Mainstream smoke particulate phase constituent yields when cigarettes are smoked with the
Massachusetts smoking regimen can be most reliably estimated from either a cigarette's
nicotine or ‘tar’ yields (estimates can be based on yields measured under either FTC or
Massachusetts smoking conditions). Mainstream smoke vapor phase constituent yields can be
estimated from a cigarette’s carbon monoxide yields under either smoking condition.
Sidestream smoke constituent yields can be estimated from cigarette tobacco weight or other
general yielé parameters, A smoke constituent form that summarizes yield estimates for a
given cigarette brand style has been designed based on spreadsheet software. The constituent
farm provides information about the cigarette brand style of interest, the prediction parameters
‘employed, estimates of the individual mainstream and sidestream smoke constituent yields
predicted by interpolation fram the functional relationships established in the 1999
Massachusetts Benchmark Study and, perhaps most importantly, an indication of the range of
constituent yields expected from similar cigarettes encompassing the range of design features
found in the 1999 Massachusetts Benchmark Study cohort. Use of such a form, based on the
functional relationships developed through this “benchmarking” study, is a practical means of
providing smoke constituent data for cigarette brands sold in Massachusetts.
