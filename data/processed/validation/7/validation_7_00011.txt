SUBJECT: Scientific Affairs Bvaluation of Consumer Complaint #92068

‘This complaint involves Salem ULT 85 cigarettes which were returned by a consumer
with a complaint that they ‘smelled like lighter fluid and seemed to flare up.” The cigareites
and the inside of the foil innerliner had a yellowish stain. Analysis by on-line purge-and-
rap/GC/MS and a list of compounds foreign to tobacco was generated, The compounds
identified alkanes, alkylated alkanes, cycloalkames, and naphthalene derivatives. The types
and proportions of these chemicals suggest that the cigarettes were contaminated with
hydrocarbons, such as those that might be found in kerosene or diesel fuel.

Kerosene is produced by fractionation of the middle petroleum distillate, The actual
composition of kerosene is not cbustant and differs widely, but consists mainly of a mixture
of linear and branched aliphatic hydracarbons, olefins, cycloparaffins and aromatic
compounds in the C10 to C16 range. Toxicological responses to kerosenes vary widely
according to their origin and composition. Deodorized and refined kerosenes are the least
toxic. Other kerosenes may contain benzene, alkyl benzene and other aromatic compounds
which may be toxic to the blood forming organs. The overall oral toxicity of kerosenes is
moderate, Ingestion results in rapid absorption resulting in gastrointestinal upset and
systemic effects such as drowsiness and CNS depression, Aspiration of kerosene into the
lungs may result in lung damage and even death. Kerosene is not highly volatile, and
therefore, it is not usually a hazard by inhalation. Direct contact with kerosene rapidly
defats the skin and may result irritation and inflammation. The recommended worker
exposure level for exposures 10 kerosene vapors set by NIOSH is 14 ppm. The toxicological
effects of diese! fuel are similar ta those of kerosene.

Since the consumer did not smoke these cigarettes, no adverse health effects would
be expected. Also, inhalation of the small quantity of vapors emanating from the pack or
cigarettes would not be expected to have any toxicological significance.
