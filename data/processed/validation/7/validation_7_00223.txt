FROM: Guy Ordaker

SUBJECT: Status of Pilot Project to investigate the Effect of ETS on the Air
Quality within Restaurants

DATE: May 9, 1986

Delivery of the briefcase sampling systems has been delayed. General
Electric, the manufacturer of the carbon monoxide (CO) monitor included in the
prototype sampling system, has decided to leave the market. This development
was unexpected by our contacts in GE's Hanufacturing Division. Consequently,
several vendors were surveyed in order to identify other CO monitors
patentiatly compatible with the prototype briefcase design. A monitor has
been identified which appears applicable, The advantages of this monitor are
smaller size and weight and easier maintenance. The monitor’s major
disadvantage is that it samples passively; thus, it will have to be modified
in order to permit active sampling. One of these monitors has been ordered on
a trial basis to confirm its applicability, Engineers responsible for
fabricating the sampling system estimate that feasibility can be assessed and
additional monitors can be ordered in approximately two weeks. In addition,
they estinate that redesign, performance tests, and fabrication will require
at most 18 weeks. Delivery of the sampling system and start of the project
are therefore expected to occur before September 1986. Purchase and delivery
of other briefcase components and supplies are on schedule.
