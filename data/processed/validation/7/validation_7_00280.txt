CARR: ™ PROMOTION IDEAS FOR DORAL AND MAGNA: WHICH ONES_
ARE BEST FOR THE BRANDS?

We have seen a significant body of promotional ideas in the past 2 weeks for DORAL and
MAGNA from Flair, W.A. Robinson and LHC. Our task is to develop a viable short list of
good ideas which would be appropriate to discuss with consumers. (Initial groups for
DORAL are 8/28-29 and 9/6-? for MAGNA).

In.an effort to help develop that list, (since DORAL'S groups are first) we forward screening
priorities specific to DORAL, and following the priorities is a recommendation of which
presented promotions are most appropriate for DORAL. We have tried to be scrupulously
objective, applying the criteria we judge to be best for the brands, regardless of the source of
the ideas,

A similar review of MAGNA promotion ideas will be forwarded next week.
DORAL

The whole role of promotions for DORAL is dependent on where we go on price. Simply
put, if we slay where we are, trial and continuity promotions in addition to OCC's will be
necessary to compete against lower-priced brands. If we lower the price to the lowest priced
tier, price alone will be the driving force, with a possible need for continuity /loyalty-based
promotions.

We are operating from the assumption that the price of DORAL will be lowered. If Pyramid
didn’t exist, we could hold out for a while and make the move first or respond to the
inevitable competitive move. The problem is that Pyramid does exist, is growing and
squelching it will require a fairly widespread OCC offensive. By so many measures, moving
DORAL to a lower price area is nat only inevitable, it is a continued evolution of the
original DORAL point-of-difference.

The promotion ideas for DORAL in 1990 should conform to several criteria. These criteria
follow, with reasons why they are important.
