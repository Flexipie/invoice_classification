Status of the joint Analytical/Chemical study of anmoniated flue-cured
tobaccos was reviewed by J. D. Fredrickson. Most of the chemical analyses
fave been completed. The data supported previous findings on the composition
of anmoniated flue-cured tobaccos. High ammonia levels are produced by ambient
ammoniation, Ammonia levels in the samples treated with aqueous ammonia and steam
varied with the amount of ammonia added and the steam exposure. Tobacco pi varied
with the anmonia level. Nicotine reduction was primarily a function of steam
exposure, The application of ammonia prior to steaming resutted in greater nicotine
reductions. Steam exposure on the porous belt at 250°F caused @ slight reduction
in sugars, whereas anbient amnoniation resulted in large reductions in sugar
levels. Aqueous ammonia and steam on the porous belt had little effect on sugar
levels.

The ammonia-sugar reaction products determined in this study were glucosamine,
nannosamine, galactosamine, the 2,5- and 2,6-deoxyfructosazines, and the 2,5- and
2,6-fructosazines. The Tevels of these products in ammoniated flue-cured tobaccos
pay be useful indicators of aumoniation conditions. High levels of aminosugars
and fructosazines were found in the samplos treated with gaseous ammonia under
ambient conditions, Aqueous ammonia and steam on the porous belt produced much
smaller quantitics of these compounds, The effect of steam alone on the levels
of these compounds was negligible, The relative quantities of the individual
aminosugars in the anoniated tobaccos were the same as those in the untreated
tobacco (glucosamine >> mannosamine > galactosamine), The estimated quantities of
the individual fructosazines varied with ammoniation conditions, The 2,5- and 2,6-
deoxy isomers were the major fructosazines in al} ten samples.
