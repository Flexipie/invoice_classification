February 16, 1981

To: Mr. Gregory Novak
From: R. C. Nordine

Subject: CATEGORIES

The following are my thoughts regarding the problem of defining
tar" categories mentioned in Herb Osmon's memo of February 10.
Although Herb's memo was prompted by WINSTON Full Flavor’s plan
to reduce their tar ievel to 15 mg,, I think the issue this
raises is broader than indicated by Herb.

Before giving you my recommendation, I’d like to comment on the
alternatives Herb laid out. First, I think it would be a mistake
to classify WINSTON Full Plavor as a low tar brand simply because
its “tax” number was 15 mg. In fact, I would not favor the
adoption of any strictly numerical definition of low tar if we
are going to ise categories for planning or strategic purposes.
Thus, I am against a “sliding scale" definition also.

The problem with a strictly numerical definition of tar categories )
is basically that it doesn't fit with brand family concepts. Por
example, WINSTON King siiould not be placed in the same tar category
as WINSTON Lights regardless of their numbers. Another type of
problem is illustrated by the fact that Virginia Slims non-menthol
is currently 16 mg. while Virginia Slims Menthe] is 15 mg. In
general, “comparable” styles of the same brand family belong in the
game “tar category while lights spin-offs belong in a differant
category than their parents.

There are also a number of practical problems that will arise if

we use a "tar" category definition strictly based on a brand's
“tar” number. Category performance (sales, consumer behavior, etc.)
will be subject to discontinuous jumps, and we will have problems
comparing current/upcoming research to previous studies.
