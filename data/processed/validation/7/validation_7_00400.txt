© — Glucose Isomerase Licensing

Additional details have been provided by the Law Department.
on our glucose isomerase licensing activities: Commercial suc-
cess has been achieved by a licensee of R&D technology. In
Noveuber 1981, a worldwide exclusive license was granted to Miles
Laboratories, Inc., for producing a glucose isomerase enzyme with
a Reynolds patented Flavobacterium microorganism isolated by Dr.
Chin K. Lee in the mid-7O's. Wiles has perfected a process for
immobilizing the enzyme, completed feeding studies, filed for FDA
approval and entered commercial production in April of this year.
Miles' 1983 sales of the immobilized glucose isomerase have been
over one aillion dollars. Dr. Nevin Meyer of Miles says he
believes the product is the best one on the market. Royalties to
Reynolds are expected to exceed $50,000 for 1983 and to be well
over $100,000 for 1984. .

© Genetics
Our basic studies on the chemistry and genetics of tobacco
leaf-surface constituents have enabled us to develop a flue-cured
tobaceo cultivar (designated AFC) with most of the aromatic prop-
erties of oriental tobacco, We grew a small quantity of this
tobacco at East Bend in the summer and have tested the cured leat
as an oriental substitute in a “typical” American blend. Control
eigarettes contained 55% flue-cured (CG2), 30% burley (KG1), and
15% oriental (TB). Test cigarettes contained 50% CG2, 30% KGL,
and 20% AFG (3rd priming). A select panel of "expert" smokers
rated the two products similar with a slight preference for the
test product. We plan to grow a much larger quantity of this
tobacco in 1984 for more rigorous evaluation. Test plots will
also be grown in Malaysia and Brazil, countries which either have |
