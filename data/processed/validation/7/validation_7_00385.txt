Comments and Questions:

1) NT is but one of many enzymes which repair DNA. The only DNA
adducts that MT repairs are methyl groups on nucleotide oxygens.
Tt is incapable of repairing bulky DNA adducts resulting from,
for example, polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons and aromatic
amines, Why has MT been singled out as a suspect for genetic
predisposition to bronchial carcinoma? Is the author
hypothesizing that methylating agents cause bronchial
carcinoma? If 60, what are the methylating agents the author
suspects?

2) Since the target cells in bronchial carcinoma are lung
epithelial cells, why is the author proposing to look at skin
fibroblasts? Wouldnt a study on the MT activity of pulmonary
epithelial cells be more relevant? Is the activity of MT in
skin f{broblasts expected to reflect activity in lung epithelial
cells? Since MI is known to be an inducible enzyme this may not
be a good assumption to make because lung epithelial activity
may be induced by environmental exposures but skin fibroblaste,
because they would never be exposed to environmental agents,
would not be induced,

3) Many cellular enzymatic processes diminish rapidly (ca. 24
hr) following isolation and placement of the cells in culture.
How long does the author culture the skin fibroblasts before
measuring MT activity? How does subculture affect the results?
Has the author measured MT activity in fresh tissue to verify
that his observed differences are real and not an artifact from
subculturing the cells, e.g. in the half life of the enzyme? .

4) Can one be sure that the reduced MT activity observed in
tumor patients is a "genetic predisposition" and not an effect
of the tumor? or tumor treatment(see # 5)?
