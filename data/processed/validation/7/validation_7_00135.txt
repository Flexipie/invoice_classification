Dear Mr. Arthur:

As we have discussed, you are providing very important
components for a new product R. J. Reynolds is developing.
Therefore, we want to reemphasize the need for tight security
on this product. Please continue to limit the number of people
in your organization who know about the component or aware of
R. J. Reynolds' involvement to those people who must know in
order to meet our needs. Also, please reemphasize to those
people that they should not discuss the component or R. 3.
Reynolds with anyone inside the company who has no need to
know, nor with anyone outside the company for any reason.

It is always possible that between now and the time we
introduce the product, you may receive an outside inquiry about
whether you are supplying materials for a new product to R. J.
Reynolds. There are any number of ways this issue can be
raised including references to the patent application.

In the case of any outside inguiry, we would suggest that your
response be something like this:

“We are aware that a patent application haa been filed
which references Union Carbide but “we will not comment.
about anything we may or may not be providing to any
company, nor will we comment about whether or not we are
doing business with any specific company.”

Essentially, what we are suggesting is that your position be
that questions about your business relationships with your
clients will not be addressed because the information is
proprietary, and that this includes questions about who is or

is not a client.
