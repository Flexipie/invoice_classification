Pesticides - Results of dicamba analyses in CG-3 and c+22
blends were reviewed. Three samples of CG-3 ranged 0.20-1.94
ppm, and C-22 ranged 0.08-0.25 ppm, When considered with CG-3
samples taken 7/13/88 (range 0.39-1.20 ppm), the data indicate
that wide variability in dicamba residues exist in the CG-3
blend. Since upper stalk Georgia tobacco has been excluded,
the data also indicate that other tobaccos in RJR inventory
also contain detectable residues of dicamha.

Dicamba and endosulfan analyses of tobacco dust and dry
reconstituted sheet confirm the results of previous analyses,
that these two chemicals do not concentrate during the
reconstitution process. Dicamba ranged from 0.29-0.37 ppm,
endesulfan ranged from 0.66-0.88, The current USDA/RJRT
guideline values for these two pesticides are 0.5 and 3.0 ppm,
respectively.

Industrial Hygiene - Ms. Jamie Huss participated in the
University of North Carolina Summer Institute for Occupational
Health and Safety in Williamsburg, VA, on August 2-5, 1988.
Ms. Huss conducted a workshop on how to conduct hazard
communication training sessions. The workshop was developed
and conducted at the request of the University of North
Carolina School of Public Health, Industrial Hygiene Group.
