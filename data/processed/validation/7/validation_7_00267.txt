ARTHUR J. STEVENS.

April 22, 1986

Mz. Herbert 8, Berman
250 Broadway
New York, BY 10007

Dear Councilperson Berman:

The proposal by Mayor Koch to restrict and
Prohibit smoking in public places, workplaces and
Public meetings in New York City is ill-conceived,
impractical, and bodes ill for the business and
economic environment of The Greatest City in the
World. Similar bans have been ineffective and
Productive of other troublesome problems in the
few other major cities in which they have been
adopted.

Governmental control of personal behavior of
the type contemplated by the suqgested legislation
is not the answer to balancing the needs of
nonsmokers and smokers, Common courtesy will best
address the matter - and cannot be legislated.

It is clear that if these measures are
adopted, the affected businesses will have no
choice but to pass increased costs on to all
customers and patrons.

I urge your detailed consideration of this
Proposal and its complete rejection,

Very truly yours,

AIS/pm

Treozerg
