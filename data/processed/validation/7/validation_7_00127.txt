Bignificance: Heating burley tobacco reeults in the formation of
volatile and semivolatile compounds that are thought to have a
pleasing sensory impact and smoothing effect. status: Burley
tobacco heated at 300 and 320 °F for 5 minutes in the three drying
zones was approved for review by the HRRC for QDA and QVS sensory
testing, The burley was cased prior to heating with high fructose
corn syrup (HFCS) replacing the corn syrup and invert sugar on a
1:1 basis, Licorice and cocoa. The burley casing level was
standard at 6%; the HFCS, 33%. Ames and smoke chemistry data,
including data for smoke furfural, indicated no areas of potential
concern for sensory testing. Next steps: Products containing the
heat-treated burley will be evaluated for improved sensory
attributes. A decision for market inclusion will require
additional testing. (R. Steele, 4374)

Significance: Barrier papers are being developed for XOV products
to prevent glycerin bleeding through the papers. status: Hercons
70, 728 and 75 were approved for investigation for thie purpose.
Next Steps: Should any of the Hercons perform in a satisfactory
manner, further toxicological review will be conducted.  (R.
Steele, 4374)

Significance: Papers with special burn-retardant properties are
being developed to prevent scorching of the substrate and to
contre burnback of tipping. status: Ultracarb was approved for
use in the development of burn-retardant papers for XDU.
Ultracarb is derived fron a natural mineral deposit of huntite and
hydromagnesite, a mixture of magnesium and calcium carbonate, in
northern Greece. Heavy metal, radionuclide content,
fibrous/orystalline material, thermogravinetric and EDX chemical
analyses indicated no potential concerns in these areas. Next
Steps: Trials are being conducted using papers containing
Uitracarb. (R. Steele, 4374)
