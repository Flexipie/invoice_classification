PROVINCE COUNTERS BOOTLEG TOBACCO

Alberta is poised to tighten up its laws in an effort to snuff out tobacco smuggling from central
Canada,

“There is quite a bit (of smuggling) now,” said Ivan Strang, Tory MLA for West Yellowhead,
who's sponsoring changes to the Tobacco Tax Act unveiled this week.

He said smugglers can make a tidy profit importing cigarettes from Ontario and Quebec, where
the tobacco products are up to $3 per carton cheaper than in Alberta,

“Tobacco taxes were reduced in those provinces several years ago to discourage bootleggers from.
running U.S. cigarettes across the border.

Bootleg cigarettes are mostly smuggled by car or truck, but in some cases they are mailed or
shipped here by courier, according to the provincial agency that tracks contraband activity.

“Typically, i's by vehicle. But this is not an area we want to get into detail about,” said Alberta
Gaming and Liquor Commission spokesman George Rodziewicz.

Changes proposed for the Tobacco Tax Act, aimed at preserving Alberta's tobacco tax revenues
by beefing up the province's enforcement powers, include:

More clearly restricting the amount of out-of-province tobacco products people import to Alberta
to five cartons of cigarettes or one kilogram of fine-cut tobacco.
