OBJECTIVE

Monitor product quality and implement cigarette construction
modifications when necessary, to ensure that the quality
objectives of the product are met.

IMPLEMENTED MODIFICATIONS

During the Ist quarter 1987, some 66 cigarette and some 27 filter
Product specifications were prepared (new or modified) .

The following main modifications were implemented:

The cigarette papers WP 70/110-6 were replaced by WP 60 /137-1,
on PHH’s Marlboro 100s cigarettes MLH01/02, to increase smoke
delivery.

The tipping papers 4M 0.15.4.5, of Ambassador cigarettes MAAO3/08
and HAKO3 produced by PMH, and MAAO4/05 and MAKOS/05, produced by
PIR, were replaced by 34 0.15.4,5 papers, to increase smoke
delivery.

The tipping papers 24/70, on FTR’s Muratti 2000 cigarettes MABO]
and HAHO1, were replaced by 23/60 papers, to increase smoke
delivery,

In January, the tipping paper of PTR's Brunette Double Filtre
BRDOL changed from 24/80 to 23/60, and the special BRD filter was
replaced by the current MAA filter. —

The tipping paper 23/60 of FTR's MLKI5/41 was replaced by a 24/40
paper, to increase smoke delivery.

The filter RTD of PM-UK's Raffles was reduced from 340 to 320 mm
we.
