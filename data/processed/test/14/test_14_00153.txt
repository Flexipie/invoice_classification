Investigation of the transference of activity into M5 and SS smoke from
“Sca labelled cigarettes is continuing. Results from smoking a series of nine
cigarettes on the total smoking machine yielded an average of 156 nci of activity
in the ash, SS gas phase, MS gas phase and butt.

A second series of six *SCa labelled cigarettes (with a 30mm cold butt)
was also smoked on the total smoking machine, Average recoveries from the ash,
5S gas phase, MS gas phase and butt were 100 nci of activity.

Recoveries from both sets of cigarettes accounted for 87% of calculated
activity values, excluding TPM pad activity from which half-life calculations are
incomplete.

The study being conducted to determine specific activity (act./g carbon)
is continuing with the Harvey furnace and glass combustion tube being replaced
by a Lindberg tube furnace and stainless steel (1" 0D) combustion tube packed with
the Harvey catalyst system, Results have been inconsistent when using either 1
combustion standards or '*CO2, This was apparently due to the lack of an expansion
volume behind the Sway Sampling valve. Trapping, purging and running times have
heen found to be critical and have been established to optimize counting efficiency.

A study was made of solvent effects on nicotine determination using a DB-5
column and an auto-injector. Methanol was found to yield the best sensitivity,
methylene chloride proved to be the most reproducible. Methanol tends to cause
tailing of alkaloid peaks on non-polar columns, while methylene chloride causes
quenching in the RC proportional counter. The two most suttable solvents for
nicotine determination were found to be methano? and ether,
