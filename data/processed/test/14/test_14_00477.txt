OVERALL GOALS AND SPECIFIC AIMS

The overall goal of this study is to identity amino acid residues in tubulin that are
hypothesized to be involved in GTP binding and/or hydrolysis and others that may be
involved in dimer-dimer interactions during microtubule assembly. In this proposal we
shall concentrate on the following aims.
1, To mutagenize residues in u-tubulin that may be involved in nucleotide
binding.
2. To change conserved cysteine residues to alanines.
3. To study the effects of these mutations on the biochemical properties of yeast
tubulin and on certain cellular microtubule-dependent processes.
