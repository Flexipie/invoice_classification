Experimental Detail:

Benzamidoxime was prepared in the usual manner with one
exception. after all the reagents were introduced into the
reaction flask, the solution was brought to a very gentle
reflux for approximately 24 hours. Previous experience
had shown that a vigorous reflux greatly decreased total
reaction time, but isolation of the pure amidoxine was
tedious and time consuming due to copious quantities of

a tarry residue. The milder conditions afforded good
purity benzamidoxime by simply evaporating the solvent

on the rotary evaporator.

The benzamidoxine (125 g.) was placed in a distilling

flask to which was added 250 ml. of acetic anhydride. The
mixture was -then distilled slowly. The early fractions
contained acetic anhydride and acetic acid. The later

cuts were PMO. Crude yield of PMO = 87.68 (white, crystalline).
