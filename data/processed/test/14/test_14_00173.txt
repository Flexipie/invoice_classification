objective

The objective of this study was to compare the composition of un-
diluted mainstream smoke of URSUS-59 to that of URSUS-35 and to
the standard reference cigarette IR4F based on selected chemical
parameters.

Experimental

URSUS mainstream smoke was generated using single-port smoking
machines (a). 1R4F mainstream smoke was generated using a 30-port
INBIFO smoking machine. Both URSUS types wore conditioned at 45 to
50 ¢ relative humidity. URSUS-59 was emoked at 15 J with an 0.8 5
on-time, while URSUS-35 was smoked at 20 J with an 1.05 on-time
Depending on the parameter determined, $ or 20 cigarettes were
smoked per sample, The data reported herein represent the means of
3 to 5 repetitions. Results are expressed as yields per cigarette.

Basic smoke parameters (TPH, nicotine, water, glycerin) as well as
selected aldehydes, nitrosamines, phenols, and hydrocarbons were
determined. The analyses of polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons,
vinylpyridine, and nitrogen oxides in the mainstream smoke of
URSUS cigarettes are underway.
