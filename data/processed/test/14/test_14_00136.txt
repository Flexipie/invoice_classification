THE INDOOR AIR INDEX (IAD)

A PRACTICAL METHOD TO RANK BUILDING INDOOR AIR POLLUTION.

Brief Proposal by

Demetrios J. Moschandreas, Ph.D.

Many building managers are aware of issues relating to potential indoor air problems, but they
do not spend moneys to investigate and, if necessary, mitigate the problem. They claim, that
present day science measures only snapshots of building conditions and can not measure the
relative magnitude of their problem. Furthermore, they claim that present day science can not
assure them that, after certain expenditures, the problem is corrected because there are no legal
limits or means to put the new conditions in perspective. Consequently, most building decision
makers await for scientific breakthroughs. Research to formulate indoor air quality standards
and to fully characterize indoor air quality, if possible, requires @ very long time. An easier
solution is to formulate a global indoor air index, say from 1 to LO { 10 the most polluted and 1
the least polluted). Like similar environmental indexes, an indoor air index will (1) delineate the
relative magnitude of indoor air pollution of a subject building relative to other buildings
istudied; (2) identify a desirable level/index, and (3) help allocate contro! resources optimally.

The objective of the proposed applied project is to formulate and apply an indoor air quality

index. This will be accomplished with the help of existing, in the literature, information.
