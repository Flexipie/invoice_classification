STATEMENT ON AMMONIA COMPOUNDS.
Philip Morris U.S.A.

Facts about Ammonia and Cigarettes

Ammonium (NH,*) salts are natural components of tobacco. Several
ammonia (NHs) related compounds ere used by Philip Morris in the
Cigarette manufacturing process. Relevant to this inquiry, the compounds
in current use by Philip Morris include: Diammonium Phosphate (DAP)
listed as Ammonium Phosphate Dibasic on the ingredients list), Urea,
Ammonium Hydroxide, and Ammonium Bicarbonate. These substances
have been identified to the Department of Health and Human Services
since 1986 as a part of the U.S. tobacco Industry ingredients ist. In
addition, six independent scientists have reviewed the usage level of
these substances in the manufacture of cigarettes and have concluded
they are not harmful to consumers.

Jn addition to their use in cigarette manufacturing, these ammonia
related compounds are.used in many other industries and are commonly
found in a variety of consumer products. Diammenium Phosphate is
used in alcoholic beverages, ice cream, ices, gelatin and puddings
Urea is a natural component of human metabolism, tobacco, and cats
and is also found in alcoholic beverages, bakery products and gelatin,
Ammonium Hydroxide is purchased as an arnmonie solution in water
and is significantly diluted prior to use. Ammonia is a naturally occurring
substance that plays a vital role in protein metabolism. Ammonium
Bicarbonate is used in food, baking powder formulations, cocoa
products, and baked goods. All four ingredients are on the FDA list of
substances generally recognized as safe (GRAS) for use in foods.

Ammonia felated compounds are used as processing aids and flavorants
in the manufacture of cigarettes. Processing aids ate added to tobacco
in the manufacturing procass and do not substantively persist in the
finished product. Flavorants are added to tobacco to enhance the
sensory acceptability of the product. As a flavorant in cigarettes, the
soluble ammonia aval in tobacco appears to correlate with the. sensory
“impact' of the smoke. Impact is better described as a feeling sensation
rather than an odor or taste. This impact sensation is comparable to the
effect one feels in the throat from the carbonation of a soft drink, the hot in
chili peppers, or the cool feeling of mint.
