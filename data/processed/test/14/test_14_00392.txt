PERIOD COVERED

WRITTEN BY + Bindler-G-N, (GNB)

An enzymatic degradation step is used in the extraction of
protein from tobacco. In order to optimize this enzyme

mediated extraction of proteins from tobacco, the activity

of the enzyme (pronase) was tested using the protease

detection kit supplied by Bic RAD, ‘The method used is

based on radial diffusion of agar gel containing milk

casein. The relative concentration of protease may be e
read directly, It was possible to detect as low a pronase
activity of 0.001 units. One unit of enzyme will hydrolyze
casein to produce a colour equivalent to 1 y mole of tyrosin

per minute at 7.5 pl at 37°C.

at 0h sw

We were not able to detect an endogenous proteolytic activity
in our tobacco extract samples.

However, the whole test is carried out in a Tris buffered
physiological saline solution at 7.2 pH. By adding

tobacco extract to the protease detection kit the activity of
the pronase of the same concentration changed, which revealed
that the enzyme had not the same activity and that it was
not working up to the optimum that it could reach.

As the conditions of the test system are obviously not the
same as those of the tobacco extract we will have to use anothe
method : (3H) tritium labelled protein (a modified method of
Bolton and Hunter) (3) to follow the activity of the enzyme.
