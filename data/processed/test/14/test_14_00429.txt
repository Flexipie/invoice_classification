SUMMARY

‘Two fon exchange resins, the resin used in the Fact F85 filter
and Duolite A-7 a product of Diamond Shamrock Chemical Co., have
been evaluated for gas phase efficiencies and flavor properties
on a Lark type sample cigarette, 29K. The Fact resin has tenta-
tively been identified as an ion exchange resin similar to Duolite
aT.

Each resin was found to be as efficient as SCCW charcoal
in HCN removal; for organic gas phase constituents both resins
were considerably less efficient than SCCW, Fact-41%, Duolite
A7-368 as compared to 68% removal for SCCW charcoal.

The two resins and SCCW charcoal produced essentially the
same smcke pH.

Flavorwise, neither of the resing were found to be Lark
like, both exhibiting higher raw green tastes.

PROGRESS DURING OCT.-NOV., 1975

Two ion exchange resins, the resin used in Fact P85 and
Duolite A7--a product of Diamond Shamrock Chemical Co., have
been evaluated for gas phase yields and flavor properties on
gample cigarette 29K. The Fact resin has tentatively been
identified as an ion exchange resin similar to Duolite A7.

The apparent densities of the two resins were, 9.38 g/cc for
Fact and 0.37 g/cc for Duolite A7; both resins were in the free
base form,

Sample 29K was a normal production Lark type cigarette
with an empty cavity and had no glue between the plugwrap and
mouthpiece paper. ‘the tip section of the filter was removed
and known weights of the resins {to give a 62.5% fill) were
added. The tip section was then refitted.

The two resins were also evaluated in a 50-50 mixture with
SCCW charcoal to determine if these resins would be compatible
with SCCW charcoal flavorwise.
