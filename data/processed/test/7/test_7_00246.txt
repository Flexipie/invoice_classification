SUBJECT: Scientific Exchange Meeting-Monsanto

FROM: D. Layten Davis

In relation to the scientific Exchange Meeting with Monsanto
which was scheduled for September 8, 1988, a planning session
was held during the evening of September 7, 1988 at the
Doubletree Hotel. ‘Those in attendance included John Maseili,
Bob Smith, Dick Antrim, Bob Moser, Chuck Sopher, and me.
Several ideas were discussed during the planning session and
agreed upon for presentation the following day. for the
potential tobacce projects, development of nicotine free
tobacco types, removal of proteins and protein fragments and
development of a tobacco genotype with Oriental, burley and
flue-cured characteristics were discussed. For the potential
Peanut projects, development of a large peanut, removal of
the seed coat, prevention of splitting and development of a
genotype with resistance to formation of aflatoxins were
covered. For Del Monte (California), projects related to
virus, fungi, insect and nematode resistance, season
extension, and herbicide resistance were discussed. The
crops included spinach, beans, corn, peppers, and tomatoes.
For Dei Monte (tropical fruits), the discussion centered
around insect (Med fly) control, disease resistance,
senescence control (pre- and post-harvest ripening) and
control of tree sex. The crops included bananas, pineapple,
mango, papaya, melons, ané berries. The Nabisco Brands
representatives decided not to present specific suggestions
since it would be difficult or impossible to control the
production of raw materials for our exclusive use.

The following morning, we were given a tour of the
laboratories and a review of the programs (plant ,
transformations-agronomic factors; insect, virus and
herbicide resistance} with some limited discussions on crop
quality and fungal diseases. Apparently this presentation
