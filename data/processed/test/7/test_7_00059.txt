FEDERAL ISSUES
’ XPORT RING

The House Subcommittee on Tobacco and Peanuts held its third hearing
June 14 on allegations of fraud in the export credit guarantee programs
{GSH 102 and 103), which provide loans to foreign governments to make
Anerican agricultural products affordable. According to government
investigators, 31 of the 66 tobacco leaf dealers that participated in the
GSM programs in the past three years blended cheaper foreign tobacco with
the American leaf in their shipments, but fraudulently represented to the
federal government that the tobacco was 100 percent Anerican. Representa-
tive Charlie Rose (D-NC), Chairman of the Subcommittee, said that another
hearing will be held to examine the possibility of tobacco export fraud in
transactions not involving GSM financing.
