Converter Foil Sales: In spite of the continued rise in aluminum
ingot costs, National Aluminum and Consolidated Aluminum have
elected to hold their 1/1/88 converter foil prices through June,
1988, If TAC chose to meet this competitive situation in order
to maintain sales volumes at Alcan and Golden Belt, we would
experience single-digit contribution margins and negative profit
margins, Understandably, it appears that neither of these
companies will be willing to pay TAC the approximate $,065/pound
premium required. This situation creates a 200M pound/month or
$360M sales/month hole in our 1988 plan. Internal and external
avenues to utilize this capacity will be investigated.

Asccast Reroll Capacity: In the wake of our withdrawal from the
conductor strip and litho market areas, we have uncommitted
capacity. for as-cast reroll of 400M pounds/month. Several
opportunities to utilize this capacity on an ingot tolling
contract basis are being investigated. The most promising of
these is a potential situation with National Aluminum which could
combine the sale of Mill 20 and possibly the Forte slitter with a
multi-year ingot tolling contract at a premium per pound price for
conversion.

Southern Plastics: Procter & Gamble notified TAC that its trial
material utilizing & DuPont heat sealable polyester sealant film
was the preferred lidding structure for all of P&G's peanut butter
jars. This, again, reinforces TAC's majority supply position,
Transition from the previous lidding structure, which had a TAC
coextruded sealant film, is complete with this week's 325M linear
yards production run. The structure change moves production from
the flexo press to Extruder III.
