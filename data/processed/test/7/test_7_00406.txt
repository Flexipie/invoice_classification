Re: REGIONAL INITIATIVE TOPLINE
RIP Volume Contribution and category Funding analysis

Concerns and recommendations re: Estimated 1991-Reqional
Initiative Program Voinne Contribution and 1993 planning.

The attached information was revised in october, 1991, While it
establishes a method and an estimate for volume contribution, it
is based npon rapidly changing criteria:

1) ‘The attached does not take into account significant regional
growth as exists in RIPs lead markets (driven
predominantly by Los Angeles). Lead markets for 1991
increased to 7.94. Nor does the attached reflect year-end
total Camel RIP CUME SOM which increased from 6.43 to 6.61.

1) ‘The total U.S. Hispanic population aged 18+ (now to be
discussed at 21+), as estimated by the U.S. Bureau of the
Census is significantly undercounted in the west and
southwest. Most estimates indicate an undercount of this
population segment of approximately 15% to 20% which
increases the estimated contributions significantly,
particularly when evaluated with regard to regional
concentrations.

2) Hispanic smoking incidence skews predominantly male.
However, according to the California Department of Health,
new information indicates increasing trends in the
incidence of smoking among Hispanic women, generally
corresponding with their length of time in the U.S.
(Possibly an additional opportunity for RU.)

3) The Hispanic market is extremely young and the majority is
increasingly under 21 years of age in most markets. Whereas
the general market assumptions are fixed if not decreasing
with regard to the targeted YAS age-pool, RIP market
demographics continue to increase in this same YAS
population.

I note these concerns specifically to émphasize the fact that
a budget focused on "fixed" 1991 research may not be adequate
given the increasing opportunities as reflected in all Hispanic

demographic indices.
