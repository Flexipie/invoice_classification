January 11, 1985

TO: Mr. G. W, McKenna
RE: PACKAGING DEVELOPMENT DIRECTION

This provides the Brand's recommended direction for the next
phase of packaging development work for VANTAGE. With your
approval, we will direct Mort hibby and May Bender accordingly.

As we discussed, the Brand Group believes that the opportunity
to make @ compelling and preemptive statement for VANTAGE should
focus on the Brand's user image benefits. We are convinced that
any product statement that we could make through packaging would
be eithe 1) not preemptive, or 2) not believable to the
target smoker. We do, however, have an opportunity to make a
statement about the prospective VANTAGE user which uniquely
addresses the image wants of 25-34 year-old smokers who are
switching from their initial brand choice, typically Marlboro.

These switchers can be described as smokers who are putting
their adolescence behind them and are beginning to reach for the
symbols and goals which characterize younger adulthood,
Berformance to them means working hard for the goal and the
excitement of seeing it pay off, not only for today, but for
their futures. More importantly, these people are not just
achievers, but doers. Success doesn't necessarily come easily
to them but rather requires an intensity of effort, and they
have the maturity and self-confidence that it takes to make it.
A prime illustration would be the comparison of Larry Bird, who
has developed the skills of a top-notch athlete through hard
work and effoft, versus a Dr. J type; a consummate achiever, but
one to whom success comes rather easily.

VANTAGE packaging should make, a statement about the VANTAGE
smoker which exemplifies his/her new approach to life. In the
same manner that a Marlboro pack says "one of the crowd,” or
Players says “young, urban professional,” the VANTAGE pack
should say “doer/mover.“ It should possess a vibrancy and sense
of “energy ahd even an attitide of assertiveness which charac-
terizes the prospective smokers’ mindset.
