R&D TECHNICAL SERVICES WEEKLY STATUS REPORT

ITEMS FOR WEERLY BRIEF

Sidestream olfactoneter Automation

Under Dan Kurtz's leadership, the sidestream olfactometer
used for sidestream smoke descriptive testing, has been upgraded
with computer contro] to automate all aspects of the testing pro~
cess (switching of the valves, puffing, sampling smoke, control
over the panelist indicator lights and voice emulation to provide
verbal direction to subjects at specific times in the evaluation
sequence). Since the programs are menu driven, testing
parameters can be specified with ease. These improvements will
make the testing method more accurate, flexible and less compli-
cated for the test administrator. In addition to the benefit of
having the sidestream olfactometer automated, we now have two
side-by-side olfactometers, making possible discrimination and
just-noticeable-difference experiments. PT&D provided the tech-
nical support and funding for this project with actual progran-
ming and installation being done by Dah Dugan, an outside
Programmer.

Extended Usage QDA Method

Herbert Stone and Joel Sidel, Tragon Corporation, presented a
review of our joint program to develop effective methods for
evaluating new technology products. As a result of this progran,
Extended Usage QDA Panels for Smokers and Non-emokers were devel~
oped. Benefits and possible applications were discussed and next
steps outlined. These panels represent a major accomplishment.
for the SED methods development research program.
