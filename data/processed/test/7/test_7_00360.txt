Subject: Meeting of the Committee on Nicotine Analogue Research

On Wednesday, November 26, the first meeting of the Committee on
Nicotine Analogue Research ({CONAR) was held. The purpose of the
committee, as we understand it, is toe organize an ex-house research
program to investigate known nicotine analogues, or to identify new
ones, that have minimal cardiovascular activity.

The time frame for organizing this program, by request of executive

management, would be first quarter, 1987. The following personnel have,

agreed to serve on the committee: Or. P.M. Lippielle, Dr. Thomas’
Perfetti, Dr. d. Den deBethizy, Dr. Stephen Sears, Pr. John Robinson,
Dr. Michael Dube, Or. Sam Simmons and Dr. John Reynolds (ex officio).

During the course of the meeting it was decided that the mest
reasonable appreach would be to organize the program around basic
research efforts, as opposed to treating a dedicated in vivo / in vitro
screening and testing program for synthetic analogues. This is because
the identification of unequivecal pharmacological and cardiovascular
endpoints is limited by the state-of-the-art knowledge of nicctine’s
effects in general. The consensus was that the final program should be
based on exploring the effects of analogues as part of a more general
effort to further define and understand the effects of nicotine.
Therefore it is desirable to make use of laboratories already funded by
RJR that are in a position te test analogues (e.g. Collins, Deadwyler,
Crooks, Vessell, etc.). At least one of these laboratories has synthetic
Capabilities as well. Tt was felt that this approach would facilitate
starting the program and minimize the time and expense asseciated with
the organization of mew and independent projects in, additional
laboratories.

It was also decided that the program would most easily be structured
as two separate, but integrated parts. One would involve the procurement
and/or synthesis of appropriate analogues; this should be supported by a
sound stucture-activity (SAR) research effort. The other would be based
on suitable testing paradigms tor the compounds obtained, relying on the
best pharmacological and cardiovascular endpoints available. The major
task of the committes, at present, is to identify both the endpoints and
laboratories that support research in the appropriate areas.

At present, the committee is gathering information Dr. Perfetti
Will research the identity and availability of existing nicotine
analogues. Dr, deBethizy will identify suitable endpoints to study
cardiovascular effects. Drs. Lippiello and Robinson will identify both
in vitro and in vivo pharmacological endpoints, focusing primarily on
ENS effects. Drs. Dube and Sears will explore the available ex-house
expertise in analogue synthesis and evaluation of compounds using
computerized SAR technology. Or. Simmons will serve as 4 consultant to
the committee in support of all of these areas.
