PRODUCTION

Clary Sage Products (Clary Sage Oil, Sclareol, Sclareolide)

The harvesting of Clary Sage and the production of Clary sage
Oil and Sclareol began on June 6. Harvesting was complete on
June 12, The early start date and short harvest period were due
to the severely reduced quantity of plant material in the field.
The Clary Sage was severely damaged by a drought condition in March,
April and May. The yield of Clary Sage Oil was 2085 pounds versus
6000 budgeted and the yield of Sclareol was 6466 pounds versus
12,800 budgeted. It is anticipated that inventories of Clary Sage
Oil and Sciareol (held as insurance against such a disastrous harvest)
will be sufficient to meet primary customer contracts,

Sclareolide production was interrupted for Clary Sage processing.
Year to date, a total of 7668 pounds have been produced or 76%
of budget.

Wheat

Harvesting of winter wheat is now complete. Yields were 508
below normal due to severe drought to April and May.

Pobacce

The tobacco crop appears exceptionally good having been under
irrigation. Topping and suckering are on schedule.

Peanuts

The planting of peanuts was delayed due to dry weather in
May. Stands are acceptable and herbicide and land plester applica-
tions are on schedule.

Soybeans
Fall soybeans have been planted and have germinated.
Watermelons

Watexmelons are in their rapid growth stage at this time.
The melons have a fair fruit set.
